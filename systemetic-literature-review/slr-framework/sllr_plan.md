## Pre-Planning Phase
**Duration**: 2-4 weeks
**Milestone**: Decision to proceed with SLR

### Objectives
- Determine if systematic review is the appropriate methodology
- Assess feasibility and resource requirements
- Assemble review team
- Conduct preliminary literature scoping

### Activities

#### 1. SLR Appropriateness Assessment
**Use the five-question legitimacy test:**

```
□ Q1: Multiple independent studies needed to answer the question?
□ Q2: Multiple studies with comparable evidence available?
□ Q3: Research question specific enough for systematic search?
□ Q4: Synthesis will provide new insights beyond individual studies?
□ Q5: Methodological rigor justified by question importance and resources?

✅ Proceed ONLY if ALL five answers are "YES"
```

**If any answer is "NO", consider alternatives:**
- Scoping Review (exploratory questions)
- Rapid Review (time-sensitive questions)
- Narrative Review (theory development)
- Primary Research (insufficient existing studies)

#### 2. Preliminary Literature Scoping
**Purpose**: Assess evidence landscape before committing to full SLR

**Steps:**
1. Conduct quick searches in 2-3 major databases
2. Review 50-100 titles/abstracts
3. Assess:
   - Volume of available literature (target: 10+ relevant studies)
   - Study design distribution
   - Geographic and temporal spread
   - Outcome measurement approaches
   - Language diversity

**Decision Point:**
- ✅ Sufficient evidence exists → Proceed
- ⚠️ Limited evidence → Consider scoping review
- ❌ Minimal evidence → Consider primary research

#### 3. Team Assembly
**Minimum Required Roles:**

| Role | Responsibilities | Qualifications |
|------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Lead Investigator** | Overall coordination, protocol development, final decisions | SLR methodology expertise |
| **Content Expert(s)** | Research question refinement, clinical/contextual guidance | Subject matter expertise |
| **Methodologist** | Quality assessment, statistical analysis, bias evaluation | Research methodology training |
| **Information Specialist** | Search strategy development, database management | Library science or systematic searching |
| **Reviewer 1 & 2** | Screening, data extraction, quality assessment | Research training, topic knowledge |

**Recommended Additional Roles:**
- Statistician (for meta-analysis)
- Patient/Stakeholder Representative (where applicable)
- Research Assistant (for administrative support)

#### 4. Feasibility Assessment

**Resource Checklist:**
```
Personnel:
□ Minimum 2 independent reviewers available
□ Methodological expertise accessible
□ Statistical support available (if meta-analysis planned)

Time:
□ 12-24 months available for completion
□ Protected time for lead investigator (minimum 20% FTE)
□ Reviewer availability throughout project

Access:
□ Access to required databases (minimum 3-4)
□ Full-text article retrieval capabilities
□ Reference management software
□ Data extraction and analysis software

Financial:
□ Database subscription costs covered
□ Software licensing funded
□ Publication fees budgeted
□ Conference presentation costs (if applicable)
```

### Deliverables
- ✅ Feasibility assessment report
- ✅ Team composition confirmed
- ✅ Preliminary evidence mapping
- ✅ Resource gap identification
- ✅ Decision to proceed documented

## Phase 1: Protocol Development
**Duration**: 6-8 weeks
**Milestone**: Complete, peer-reviewed protocol ready for registration

### Objectives
- Develop comprehensive, pre-defined protocol
- Address all Seven Essential Pillars
- Achieve team consensus on methodology
- Prepare for protocol registration

### Section 1.1: Research Question Formulation (Week 1-2)

#### Activity: Structured Question Development

**Choose Appropriate Framework:**

| Review Type | Framework | Components |
|-------------|-----------|------------|
| Intervention Studies | **PICO** | Population, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome |
| Qualitative Synthesis | **SPIDER** | Sample, Phenomenon of Interest, Design, Evaluation, Research type |
| Observational Studies | **PEO** | Population, Exposure, Outcome |
| Diagnostic Studies | **PIRD** | Population, Index test, Reference standard, Diagnosis |
| Prognostic Studies | **CHARMS** | Critical appraisal and data extraction framework |

**Example - PICO Development:**

```markdown
Research Question:
"What is the effectiveness of cognitive behavioral therapy compared to 
pharmacotherapy in reducing anxiety symptoms in adults with generalized 
anxiety disorder?"

PICO Breakdown:
P (Population): Adults (18-65 years) with clinically diagnosed GAD
I (Intervention): Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
   - Individual or group format
   - 8-20 sessions
   - Manualized protocols
C (Comparison): Pharmacotherapy
   - SSRIs or SNRIs
   - Therapeutic dosages
   - Minimum 8 weeks treatment
O (Outcome): 
   Primary: Anxiety symptom reduction (standardized scales: GAD-7, HAM-A)
   Secondary: Quality of life, functional impairment
   Timepoint: 6-12 months follow-up
```

#### Activity: Objectives Hierarchy

**Primary Objective:**
```
To systematically review and synthesize evidence on the comparative 
effectiveness of CBT versus pharmacotherapy for reducing anxiety symptoms 
in adults with GAD at 6-12 months follow-up.
```

**Secondary Objectives:**
1. Examine differential effects across patient subgroups (age, gender, severity)
2. Assess quality of life and functional outcomes
3. Evaluate treatment acceptability and dropout rates
4. Explore dose-response relationships where data permits

**Exploratory Objectives:**
1. Investigate long-term effectiveness beyond 12 months
2. Examine combination therapy effects
3. Assess cost-effectiveness where data available

### Section 1.2: Eligibility Criteria Development (Week 2)

#### Inclusion Criteria Template

```markdown
POPULATION:
✅ Adults aged 18-65 years
✅ Clinically diagnosed GAD using standardized criteria (DSM-5, ICD-11)
✅ Any setting (community, clinical, hospital)
✅ Any geographic location

INTERVENTION:
✅ Structured CBT protocols (individual or group)
✅ Minimum 8 sessions
✅ Delivered by trained therapists
✅ Clearly described intervention protocols

COMPARISON:
✅ Pharmacotherapy (SSRIs, SNRIs, benzodiazepines)
✅ Therapeutic dosages clearly reported
✅ Minimum 8 weeks treatment duration

OUTCOMES:
✅ Anxiety symptoms measured with validated instruments
✅ GAD-7, HAM-A, STAI, or equivalent scales
✅ Follow-up assessment at 6-12 months
✅ Sufficient statistical data for synthesis

STUDY DESIGN:
✅ Randomized Controlled Trials (RCTs)
✅ Quasi-experimental studies with control groups
✅ Published in peer-reviewed journals
✅ Available in English, Indonesian, or with validated translations

OTHER:
✅ Published 2000-2024 (justified by treatment evolution)
✅ Full-text available
✅ Sufficient methodological detail reported
```

#### Exclusion Criteria Template

```markdown
POPULATION:
❌ Children and adolescents (<18 years)
❌ Older adults (>65 years) - different treatment response profiles
❌ Comorbid severe mental disorders (psychosis, bipolar disorder)
❌ Substance use disorders as primary diagnosis

INTERVENTION:
❌ Unstructured counseling or support groups
❌ Self-help CBT without therapist guidance
❌ Interventions <8 sessions
❌ Combined treatment protocols (confounding effects)

STUDY DESIGN:
❌ Case studies, case series (n<10)
❌ Observational studies without control groups
❌ Systematic reviews and meta-analyses (use for reference screening)
❌ Conference abstracts without full publications
❌ Dissertation/thesis without peer review

OTHER:
❌ Animal studies
❌ Studies without extractable outcome data
❌ Duplicate publications (keep most comprehensive)
```

### Section 1.4: Selection Process Design (Week 4)

#### Two-Stage Screening Protocol

**Stage 1: Title and Abstract Screening**

```markdown
Screening Procedure:
1. Import all search results to reference manager (EndNote, Zotero)
2. Remove duplicates using automated and manual methods
3. Export to screening software (Covidence, Rayyan, DistillerSR)
4. Two reviewers independently screen ALL titles/abstracts
5. Apply broad inclusion criteria at this stage (when in doubt, include)
6. Tag exclusion reasons using predefined categories
7. Resolve conflicts through discussion or third reviewer
8. Document screening decisions and inter-rater agreement

Liberal Inclusion at Stage 1:
- If title/abstract insufficient to determine eligibility → INCLUDE
- If any doubt about exclusion → INCLUDE
- Conservative exclusion only for clearly irrelevant studies
```

**Stage 2: Full-Text Screening**

```markdown
Full-Text Review Procedure:
1. Retrieve full-text articles for all included from Stage 1
2. Two reviewers independently assess full-text against criteria
3. Apply strict inclusion/exclusion criteria
4. Document specific reasons for exclusion
5. Attempt to obtain missing full-texts (contact authors)
6. Resolve conflicts through discussion or third reviewer
7. Create final included studies list
8. Prepare PRISMA flow diagram

Exclusion Documentation:
For each excluded study record:
- Study ID (Author, Year)
- Primary reason for exclusion
- Secondary reasons (if applicable)
- Reviewer initials and date
```

#### Inter-Rater Reliability Assessment

```markdown
Calculate Cohen's Kappa (κ) for screening agreement:

Interpretation:
κ < 0.20  = Poor agreement → Retrain reviewers
κ 0.21-0.40 = Fair agreement → Clarify criteria
κ 0.41-0.60 = Moderate agreement → Acceptable with discussion
κ 0.61-0.80 = Substantial agreement → Good
κ > 0.80 = Almost perfect agreement → Excellent

Target: κ ≥ 0.60 for title/abstract screening
        κ ≥ 0.80 for full-text screening

If below targets:
- Review and clarify inclusion/exclusion criteria
- Conduct additional training
- Pilot test with sample until agreement improves
```

#### Conflict Resolution Protocol

```markdown
Level 1: Reviewer Discussion
- Two reviewers discuss discrepancies
- Review eligibility criteria together
- Attempt consensus through discussion
- Document decision rationale

Level 2: Third Reviewer Adjudication
- If consensus not reached, involve third reviewer
- Third reviewer independently assesses
- All three reviewers discuss
- Majority decision or consensus required

Level 3: Full Team Consultation
- For complex or precedent-setting decisions
- Present to full review team
- Documented team decision
- May inform criteria clarification
```

### Section 1.5: Quality Assessment Planning (Week 5)

#### Quality Assessment Tool Selection

**For RCTs: Cochrane Risk of Bias Tool 2 (RoB 2)**

```markdown
Domains Assessed:
1. Bias arising from randomization process
2. Bias due to deviations from intended interventions
3. Bias due to missing outcome data
4. Bias in measurement of outcomes
5. Bias in selection of reported results

Risk Levels:
- Low risk
- Some concerns
- High risk

Overall Judgment: Based on domain assessments
```

**For Non-Randomized Studies: ROBINS-I**

```markdown
Domains Assessed:
1. Bias due to confounding
2. Bias in selection of participants
3. Bias in classification of interventions
4. Bias due to deviations from intended interventions
5. Bias due to missing data
6. Bias in measurement of outcomes
7. Bias in selection of reported results
```

#### Quality Assessment Process

```markdown
Procedure:
1. Two reviewers independently assess each study
2. Complete assessment forms for all included studies
3. Rate each domain according to tool criteria
4. Provide justifications and supporting quotes
5. Determine overall risk of bias rating
6. Compare assessments and resolve discrepancies
7. Document final consensus ratings

Training:
- Review assessment tool manual
- Complete practice assessments (5-10 studies)
- Achieve ≥80% agreement before proceeding
- Regular calibration sessions during assessment
```

#### Quality Integration Strategy

```markdown
How Quality Assessment Will Inform Review:

Synthesis:
- Stratify meta-analysis by quality level
- Conduct sensitivity analysis excluding high-risk studies
- Weight studies by quality in narrative synthesis

Interpretation:
- Discuss quality limitations in findings
- Assess certainty of evidence (GRADE approach)
- Qualify conclusions based on overall quality

Reporting:
- Present quality assessment summary table
- Include risk of bias graphs
- Discuss quality as limitation where appropriate
```

#### Pilot Testing Process

```markdown
Pilot Test Protocol:
1. Select 3-5 diverse included studies
2. Both reviewers independently extract data
3. Compare extractions and identify discrepancies
4. Clarify extraction rules and form instructions
5. Modify form as needed
6. Repeat until ≥90% agreement achieved
7. Document final form and instructions

Common Pilot Issues to Address:
- Ambiguous data fields
- Unclear extraction rules
- Missing relevant data categories
- Over-complicated extraction
- Insufficient instruction clarity
```

#### Missing Data Management

```markdown
Strategy for Missing or Unclear Data:

Level 1: Check Supplementary Materials
- Review online appendices
- Check trial registry entries
- Review protocol publications

Level 2: Contact Study Authors
- Email template prepared
- Request specific missing data
- Allow 2 weeks for response
- Send one reminder if no response
- Document all correspondence

Level 3: Calculation/Estimation
- Calculate from reported statistics when possible
- Use established estimation methods
- Clearly document all calculations
- Note limitations in synthesis

Level 4: Exclusion from Specific Analyses
- Include study but exclude from analyses requiring missing data
- Document impact on synthesis
- Discuss as limitation
```

### Section 1.7: Synthesis Planning (Week 6-7)

#### Meta-Analysis Feasibility Assessment

**Criteria for Meta-Analysis:**
```markdown
✅ Proceed with Meta-Analysis IF:
- ≥3 studies with comparable populations
- Similar intervention and comparison conditions
- Same outcome measure or convertible scales
- Sufficient statistical data (means, SDs, n)
- Acceptable clinical heterogeneity

⚠️ Use Narrative Synthesis IF:
- High clinical heterogeneity
- Diverse outcome measures
- Insufficient statistical data
- <3 comparable studies
```

#### Statistical Analysis Plan

**If Meta-Analysis Feasible:**

```markdown
Effect Size Calculation:
- Primary: Standardized Mean Difference (SMD, Hedges' g)
- Rationale: Different anxiety scales used across studies
- Interpretation: 0.2 small, 0.5 medium, 0.8 large effect

Meta-Analysis Model:
- Random-effects model (DerSimonian & Laird)
- Rationale: Expected heterogeneity across studies
- Sensitivity: Test fixed-effect model for comparison

Heterogeneity Assessment:
- I² statistic:
  * 0-40%: Low heterogeneity
  * 30-60%: Moderate heterogeneity
  * 50-90%: Substantial heterogeneity
  * 75-100%: Considerable heterogeneity
- Chi² test (p < 0.10 indicates significant heterogeneity)
- Tau² (between-study variance)

Subgroup Analyses (if ≥10 studies):
- Age groups (18-30, 31-50, 51-65)
- Gender (if gender-specific data available)
- GAD severity (mild/moderate vs. severe)
- CBT format (individual vs. group)
- CBT duration (<12 vs. ≥12 sessions)

Sensitivity Analyses:
- Exclude high risk of bias studies
- Exclude outliers (if identified)
- Fixed vs. random effects comparison
- Different effect size calculations

Publication Bias Assessment (if ≥10 studies):
- Funnel plot visual inspection
- Egger's regression test
- Trim-and-fill method
- Fail-safe N calculation

Software:
- RevMan 5.4 (Cochrane software)
- R (metafor package) for advanced analyses
- STATA (meta-analysis commands)
```

**If Narrative Synthesis Required:**

```markdown
Narrative Synthesis Framework:

Step 1: Develop Preliminary Synthesis
- Tabulate study characteristics
- Group studies by similarity
- Identify patterns in findings

Step 2: Explore Relationships
- Examine factors influencing effectiveness
- Assess consistency across studies
- Identify moderators and mediators

Step 3: Assess Robustness
- Consider methodological quality impact
- Evaluate sensitivity to assumptions
- Test synthesis conclusions

Step 4: Synthesize Findings
- Develop narrative summary
- Use vote counting (with caution)
- Create conceptual framework
- Generate conclusions and implications

Tools:
- Harvest plots
- Albatross plots
- Summary tables
- Logic models
```

#### GRADE Assessment Planning

```markdown
Assess Certainty of Evidence Using GRADE:

Starting Level (Study Design):
- RCTs: HIGH certainty
- Observational: LOW certainty

Downgrade for:
- Risk of bias (−1 or −2 levels)
- Inconsistency (−1 or −2 levels)
- Indirectness (−1 or −2 levels)
- Imprecision (−1 or −2 levels)
- Publication bias (−1 level)

Upgrade for (observational only):
- Large magnitude of effect (+1 or +2 levels)
- Dose-response gradient (+1 level)
- Plausible confounding (+1 level)

Final Certainty Levels:
⊕⊕⊕⊕ HIGH: Very confident in effect estimate
⊕⊕⊕◯ MODERATE: Moderately confident
⊕⊕◯◯ LOW: Limited confidence
⊕◯◯◯ VERY LOW: Very little confidence
```

## Phase 2: Protocol Registration
**Duration**: 1-2 weeks
**Milestone**: Protocol publicly registered

### Objectives
- Register protocol in appropriate platform
- Establish public record of methodology
- Enable tracking and transparency
- Prevent selective reporting

### Platform Selection

**PROSPERO (International Prospective Register of Systematic Reviews)**
```markdown
Appropriate For:
✅ Health-related systematic reviews
✅ Reviews of interventions, diagnosis, prognosis, etiology
✅ International recognition

Requirements:
- Detailed protocol information
- No commercial interests
- Review not substantially complete (>50% data extraction)

URL: https://www.crd.york.ac.uk/prospero/
```

**Alternative Platforms:**
```markdown
Open Science Framework (OSF):
- Multidisciplinary
- More flexible than PROSPERO
- Pre-registration and version control
URL: https://osf.io/

Cochrane Library:
- For Cochrane systematic reviews
- Rigorous editorial oversight
URL: https://www.cochranelibrary.com/

protocols.io:
- Protocol sharing and version control
- DOI assignment
URL: https://www.protocols.io/
```

### Registration Process

**Step 1: Prepare Registration Information**
```markdown
Required Fields (PROSPERO example):
1. Review title
2. Named contact and contributors
3. Review question(s)
4. Searches (sources, dates, restrictions)
5. Condition or domain
6. Participants/population
7. Intervention(s)/exposure(s)
8. Comparator(s)/control
9. Types of study
10. Context
11. Primary outcome(s)
12. Secondary outcome(s)
13. Data extraction
14. Risk of bias assessment
15. Strategy for data synthesis
16. Analysis of subgroups or subsets
17. Dissemination plans
18. Conflicts of interest
19. Funding
```

**Step 2: Complete Registration Form**
```markdown
Tips for Completion:
- Be specific and comprehensive
- Copy from protocol document (consistency)
- Include all databases to be searched
- Specify exact quality assessment tools
- Describe synthesis methods in detail
- Note any planned amendments process

Common Errors to Avoid:
- Vague research questions
- Incomplete search strategy description
- Missing quality assessment details
- Unclear synthesis plans
- Insufficient outcome definitions
```

**Step 3: Submit and Track**
```markdown
After Submission:
- Registration typically processed within 10 business days
- Receive PROSPERO ID (CRD42XXXXXXXXX)
- Registration becomes publicly searchable
- Update protocol if substantive changes occur

Record Keeping:
- Save registration confirmation
- Note registration number in all documents
- Include in manuscript submissions
- Update if protocol amended
```

### Protocol Amendment Management

**When to Amend Protocol:**
```markdown
Required Amendments:
- Changes to eligibility criteria
- Additional databases searched
- Modified outcome measures
- Different quality assessment tools
- Changed synthesis methods

Documentation Required:
- Date of amendment
- Description of change
- Rationale for change
- Potential impact on review
- Approval by review team

Amendment Process:
1. Document proposed change and rationale
2. Obtain team consensus
3. Update protocol document (version control)
4. Update registration (if substantive)
5. Report amendments in final review
```

### Deliverables
- ✅ Protocol registered on appropriate platform
- ✅ Registration number obtained
- ✅ Protocol version control system established
- ✅ Amendment tracking process in place

### Meta-Analysis Approach (if feasible)

**Week 1: Data Preparation and Effect Size Calculation**

```markdown
Effect Size Calculations:

For Continuous Outcomes (anxiety scores):
Calculate Standardized Mean Difference (Hedges' g):
- Preferred over Cohen's d (unbiased for small samples)
- Formula: g = (M1 - M2) / SDpooled × correction factor
- Direction: Negative = favors CBT (anxiety reduction)

Required Data per Comparison:
- Group 1 (CBT): M1, SD1, n1
- Group 2 (Medication): M2, SD2, n2

If Means/SDs Missing:
- Calculate from median, range, IQR (Wan et al. 2014 methods)
- Derive SD from CI or p-value
- Estimate from t-statistic or F-statistic
- Document all conversions

Calculate Standard Error (SE):
SE = √[(n1+n2)/(n1×n2) + g²/(2(n1+n2))]

Calculate 95% CI:
Lower CI = g - 1.96 × SE
Upper CI = g + 1.96 × SE

For Dichotomous Outcomes (response/remission rates):
Calculate Risk Ratio (RR) or Odds Ratio (OR):
- Extract: events and total n per group
- Calculate effect size and SE
- Compute 95% CI

Software for Calculations:
- RevMan 5.4 (built-in calculator)
- R (metafor package, escalc function)
- Excel with meta-analysis formulas
```

Publication Bias Assessment:

If ≥10 studies, conduct:

1. Funnel Plot:
   - Visual inspection
   - Symmetry suggests no bias
   - Asymmetry may indicate bias OR heterogeneity OR chance

   - Rosenthal's method
   - Orwin's method (specify meaningful effect)

## Troubleshooting Common Challenges

### Challenge 1: Insufficient Studies Found

**Problem:** Preliminary or full search yields <3 relevant studies

**Solutions:**
1. **Broaden inclusion criteria** (if justified):
   - Expand age range
   - Include additional study designs
   - Broaden intervention definitions
   - Consider related conditions

2. **Reconsider review type**:
   - Switch to scoping review
   - Conduct narrative review
   - Write perspective/commentary piece

3. **Refine question**:
   - Narrow to specific sub-question with more studies
   - Combine related interventions or outcomes

**Prevention:**
- Conduct thorough preliminary scoping
- Be realistic about evidence availability
- Consult content experts early

### Challenge 2: Overwhelming Number of Results

**Problem:** Search yields 10,000+ records

**Solutions:**
1. **Refine search strategy**:
   - Add specificity to search terms
   - Use additional filters (dates, study design)
   - Combine concepts more precisely

2. **Narrow scope**:
   - Focus on specific population subgroup
   - Limit to particular intervention type
   - Restrict to certain outcomes

3. **Increase efficiency**:
   - Use machine learning screening tools (if available)
   - Prioritize screening by relevance ranking
   - Add more screening rounds

**Prevention:**
- Pilot test search strategy
- Balance sensitivity and precision
- Consider search filters early

### Challenge 3: High Heterogeneity (I² > 75%)

**Problem:** Statistical heterogeneity prevents meaningful meta-analysis

**Solutions:**
1. **Investigate sources**:
   - Conduct subgroup analyses
   - Examine outliers
   - Look for methodological differences

2. **Adjust synthesis approach**:
   - Use narrative synthesis instead
   - Present results descriptively
   - Create summary tables rather than forest plots

3. **Stratify analyses**:
   - Separate meta-analyses by source of heterogeneity
   - Present results for homogeneous subgroups

**Prevention:**
- Plan for heterogeneity in protocol
- Pre-specify subgroup analyses
- Consider narrative synthesis from outset if diversity expected

### Challenge 4: Missing Data from Studies

**Problem:** Insufficient data for meta-analysis despite eligible studies

**Solutions:**
1. **Contact authors**:
   - Send clear, specific requests
   - Allow adequate response time
   - Follow up once

2. **Calculate from available information**:
   - Use statistical conversion formulas
   - Estimate SDs from other studies
   - Derive effect sizes from test statistics

3. **Report limitations**:
   - Document what data is missing
   - Conduct sensitivity analyses
   - Discuss impact on conclusions

**Prevention:**
- Start author contact early
- Be prepared for non-response
- Have backup synthesis plan

### Challenge 5: Team Member Disagreement

**Problem:** Frequent screening/extraction conflicts or major methodological disagreements

**Solutions:**
1. **Clarify criteria**:
   - Hold calibration meetings
   - Create decision rules document
   - Provide additional examples

2. **Additional training**:
   - Review tools and guidelines together
   - Complete practice exercises
   - Discuss interpretations

3. **Structured resolution**:
   - Document rationale for decisions
   - Involve third reviewer systematically
   - Establish clear decision protocol

**Prevention:**
- Extensive pilot testing
- Regular team meetings
- Clear communication protocols

### Challenge 6: Timeline Delays

**Problem:** Review taking longer than planned

**Solutions:**
1. **Prioritize critical path**:
   - Focus on tasks that block others
   - Delay non-essential components
   - Adjust scope if necessary

2. **Increase resources**:
   - Add team members if possible
   - Extend protected time
   - Outsource specific tasks (e.g., data entry)

3. **Adjust expectations**:
   - Revise timeline realistically
   - Communicate with stakeholders
   - Plan for longer duration

**Prevention:**
- Build buffer time into timeline
- Monitor progress regularly
- Address delays early

## Conclusion

This comprehensive plan provides a complete roadmap for conducting a systematic literature review from initial conception through publication and dissemination. Success requires:

1. **Rigorous Planning**: Invest time in protocol development
2. **Methodological Adherence**: Follow the Seven Essential Pillars
3. **Team Collaboration**: Clear roles and communication
4. **Systematic Documentation**: Maintain complete audit trail
5. **Quality Focus**: Multiple checkpoints and verification
6. **Realistic Timelines**: Allow adequate time for each phase
7. **Flexibility**: Adapt to challenges while maintaining rigor

**Key Success Factors:**
- Start with clear, answerable research questions
- Ensure adequate resources and expertise
- Maintain independence and dual review throughout
- Document every decision transparently
- Plan for heterogeneity and missing data
- Allow sufficient time (typically 18-24 months)
- Engage stakeholders throughout the process

**Remember:**
> "A systematic literature review is a marathon, not a sprint. Quality and rigor should never be sacrificed for speed. The time invested in proper methodology will yield credible, influential findings that advance knowledge and inform practice."

**Final Checklist Before Starting Your SLR:**
□ Research question appropriate for systematic review
□ Evidence base sufficient (≥10 expected studies)
□ Resources adequate (time, personnel, funding)
□ Expertise available (methodology, content, statistical)
□ Institutional support secured
□ Timeline realistic (12-24 months)
□ Commitment to rigorous methodology confirmed

If all boxes checked: **Proceed with confidence!**

If any boxes unchecked: **Address gaps before beginning.**
