# Screening Inclusion and Exclusion Criteria

This document details the rigorous screening criteria applied to the initial pool of 405 unique records to arrive at the final selection of 39 papers for the Systematic Literature Review. The screening process followed a **Two-Stream Strategy**: Stream A for Indonesian Context and Stream B for Global Intervention Design.

## 1. Universal Exclusion Criteria (Applied to All Papers)

Before applying stream-specific rules, the following papers were excluded universally:

| Category | Exclusion Criteria |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Wrong Publication Type** | • Reviews (Systematic reviews, Scoping reviews, Meta-analyses, Overviews, Landscape analyses)<br>• Protocols (Study protocols, Trial designs without results)<br>• Conference Abstracts (where full text is unavailable)<br>• Editorials, Commentaries, Letters |
| **Wrong Technology** | • Hospital Information Systems (HIS) / Electronic Medical Records (EMR) for billing/admin only<br>• Telemedicine "carts" or robots (video-only hardware)<br>• Apps not involving mobile messaging/chat (e.g., pure data collection tools) |
| **Wrong Population** | • Medical/Nursing Student Education (e.g., "WhatsApp for curriculum delivery")<br>• Doctor-to-Doctor communication only (no patient involvement) |
| **Wrong Topic** | • Pure clinical prevalence surveys with no technology component<br>• Topics unrelated to health service delivery (e.g., Climate Change, Road Safety, Supply Chain) |

---

## 2. Stream A: Context (Indonesia)

**Objective:** To understand the local digital health landscape, user behavior, and the role of community intermediaries.

### Inclusion Criteria
*   **Setting:** Must be explicitly conducted in **Indonesia**.
*   **Focus:**
    *   **Community Intermediaries:** Explicit mention of *Kader*, *Posyandu*, *Puskesmas*, *Midwives* (*Bidan*), or *Community Health Workers*.
    *   **Social Technology:** Explicit use of *WhatsApp*, *Social Media Groups*, *Chat Groups*, or *Instant Messaging* for health coordination or education.
    *   **User Behavior:** Studies on digital literacy, trust, misinformation (hoaxes), or privacy *in the context of health*.

### Exclusion Criteria
*   **COVID-19 Surveys:** Purely cross-sectional surveys on "mental health during the pandemic" or "acceptance of COVID apps" without an actual intervention or active usage component.
*   **Generic Apps:** Studies mentioning "mobile health apps" generally without specifying a social/chat/community component.

---

## 3. Stream B: Design (Global)

**Objective:** To identify design principles for interactive NCD interventions using widely accessible chat platforms.

### Inclusion Criteria
*   **Setting:** Global (excluding High-Income Countries *unless* focusing on migrant populations or trust frameworks).
*   **Disease Focus:** Strictly limited to **Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs)**:
    *   Diabetes (Type 2)
    *   Hypertension
    *   Cardiovascular Disease (CVD) / Heart Failure
    *   Obesity / Weight Management
*   **Technology:**
    *   **Specific Platforms:** *WhatsApp*, *WeChat*, *LINE*, *Telegram*, *Messenger*, *Viber*.
    *   **Interaction:** Must involve **Two-way / Bi-directional communication** (e.g., counseling, coaching, peer support, discussion groups, interactive feedback).

### Exclusion Criteria
*   **Wrong Disease:** HIV, Malaria, Tuberculosis (TB), Maternal/Child Health (unless comorbid with NCD). *Note: These topics were allowed in Stream A (Indonesia) but excluded in Stream B (Global).*
*   **Old/Passive Technology:**
    *   One-way SMS reminders (unidirectional broadcasts).
    *   Automated "text messaging" without interaction.
    *   Technology predating 2015.
*   **High-Tech:** Interventions requiring expensive custom apps, VR, or AI diagnostics not accessible via standard chat platforms.

---

## Final Selection Summary

Applying these criteria resulted in the final selection of **39 papers**:

*   **18 Papers in Stream A (Context):** High-quality studies from Indonesia focusing on the role of Kaders, Posyandu, and the specific use of WhatsApp/Social Media for health coordination.
*   **21 Papers in Stream B (Design):** Global studies demonstrating interactive, two-way interventions for NCD management (Diabetes, Hypertension) using standard chat platforms (WhatsApp, WeChat).
