# Authoritative Digital Health Sources
# Reference guide for global digital health intelligence gathering

## Global Health Authorities

### World Health Organization (WHO)
- **Coverage**: Global health policies, digital health guidelines, telemedicine standards
- **Website**: https://www.who.int/health-topics/digital-health
- **Value**: Official global health authority, sets international standards
- **Use for**: Policy analysis, global health framework understanding

### Healthcare Information and Management Systems Society (HIMSS)
- **Coverage**: Healthcare IT standards, electronic health records, health information systems
- **Website**: https://www.himss.org/
- **Value**: Official healthcare IT standards body
- **Use for**: Implementation standards, technology requirements analysis

### Digital Health Authority (DHA)
- **Coverage**: Digital health regulations, certification processes, telehealth guidelines
- **Website**: https://www.digitalhealth.gov/
- **Value**: US-based but provides global digital health best practices
- **Use for**: Regulatory compliance understanding, technology assessment

## Government & Policy Sources

### Health Ministry Directories
- **Search Strategy**: "[Country] Ministry of Health digital health announcements"
- **Value**: Official policy announcements, regulatory updates
- **Examples**:
  - "Indonesia Ministry of Health digital health announcements"
  - "Singapore Ministry of Health digital health policy"
  - "Thailand FDA digital health guidelines"

### Health Agency Websites

## Search Best Practices

When searching for digital health intelligence:

1. **Prioritize Recent Timeframes**:
   - Last 7 days: Breaking developments, funding announcements
   - Last 30 days: Market trends, technology adoption
   - Last 90 days: Strategic overview, market analysis

2. **Use Authoritative Sources First**:
   - Start with WHO and national health authority sources
   - Complement with industry research for validation
   - Cross-reference multiple sources for comprehensive coverage

3. **Geographic Targeting**:
   - Use country-specific terms for local regulatory information
   - Combine global trends with regional implementation requirements
   - Consider cultural and economic context in analysis

4. **Quality Assessment**:
   - Evaluate source credibility and authority level
   - Check publication dates for current relevance
   - Verify regulatory standing and policy implementation track record

5. **Competitive Intelligence**:
   - Include market sizing and growth projections
   - Analyze technology adoption rates and barriers
   - Consider partnership ecosystem development activities
