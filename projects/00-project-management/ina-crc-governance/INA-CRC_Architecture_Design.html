Phase I: Strategic Initiation and Study Opportunity Search

In this phase, INA-CRC plays a strategic role as a matchmaker and single entry point to attract global studies to Indonesia.

Key Process Actors INA-CRC Role/System Regulatory Basis/INA-CRC Tasks
1.1. Study Submission Sponsor/CRO Sponsor/CRO contacts INA-CRC (Single Entry Point) and presents clinical trial plan (Study Opps).
Task
Finding sponsors and global clinical research cooperation
1.2. Initial CRU Selection INA-CRC CRU INA-CRC coordinates with CRUs meeting criteria. CRUs propose Key Opinion Leaders (KOL) as Principal Investigators (PI).
Strategy

Phase II: Feasibility Study - Digital Assessment

INA-CRC uses a Digital Feasibility Platform to conduct initial feasibility assessment of CRUs/sites digitally through systematic workflows and real-time monitoring.

Key Process Actors Digital Feasibility System Architecture Key INA-CRC Data

Phase III: Legal, Ethical, and Regulatory Approval (PPUK)

This is the formal preparation phase toward study implementation, closely guided by INA-CRC to ensure governance compliance.

Key Process Actors Related Role/System Regulatory Basis/INA-CRC Tasks
3.1. Contract Negotiation Sponsor/CRO CRU INA-CRC Negotiation of Clinical Trial Agreement (CTA) and Material Transfer Agreement (MTA). INA-CRC accompanies this process; individual negotiation by researchers is prohibited.
Task
Assisting CRUs in preparing Clinical Trial Agreements. CTA must be documented.
3.2. Ethical Approval CRU KEPK CRU submits ethical approval to Health Research Ethics Committee (KEPK). PPUK must be given after obtaining Ethical Committee approval. Clinical research must be based on three general ethical principles (respect for human dignity, doing good, and justice).
3.3. PPUK Submission Sponsor/CRO CRU BPOM/Kemenkes Sponsor/CRO submits Clinical Trial Implementation Approval (PPUK). Submitted through SIAP-UK (BPOM) for drugs/vaccines or Kemenkes (for medical devices).
Requirement
Clinical Trials conducted in Indonesia must obtain PPUK from BPOM. PPUK is valid for 2 years.
3.4. PPUK Evaluation BPOM BPOM evaluates Clinical Trial documents through examination and assessment, using time to respond mechanism (maximum 20 Days). BPOM may include experts in evaluation. In emergency conditions, Drug evaluation is issued within 15 Days.
3.5. INA-CRR Registration (Initial) CRU INA-CRC CRU must register research through INA-CRR (https://ina-crr.kemkes.go.id/) for research that has received ethical approval.
Task
Managing clinical trial registration/clinical research registration. INA-CRR must be integrated with SIKN.

Phase IV: Clinical Trial Execution

Study implementation must comply with Good Clinical Practice (GCP), which are international ethical and scientific standards.

Key Process Actors Regulatory Requirements & INA-CRC BPOM/Kemenkes Regulations
4.1. Pre-Implementation (SIV) CRO/Sponsor CRU Sponsor/CRO conducts Site Readiness Visit (SIV) and protocol/GCP training. CRU must have GCP-certified PI. Clinical Trials must be conducted in Clinical Trial Centers meeting criteria.
4.2. Subject Recruitment Research Team Researchers conduct recruitment and screening according to protocol inclusion/exclusion criteria. Informed Consent must be obtained voluntarily from subjects. Rights, safety, and welfare of Clinical Trial Subjects are the most important consideration.
4.3. Performance Monitoring CRA Sponsor/CRO INA-CRC CRA monitors study implementation onsite or remotely. INA-CRC conducts CRU development and periodic monitoring/evaluation. Monitoring must ensure compliance with protocol, GCP, and regulations.
4.4. Safety Reporting Principal Investigator Sponsor Principal Investigator reports Serious Adverse Events (SAEs) to Sponsor (max. 24 hours) and Ethics Committee (max. 3 calendar days). Sponsor must report SAEs (for Phase I/vaccines/high risk) to BPOM. Reporting of Serious Side Effects of Investigational Products from Clinical Trials in Indonesia to BPOM: 7 calendar days for initial report (death/life-threatening) and 15 days for other serious effects.

GCP Compliance

Safety Monitoring

Phase V: Closure and Data Accountability

This phase focuses on study completion, audit, and submission of final data for national supervision and policy.

Key Process Actors Regulatory Requirements & INA-CRC BPOM/Kemenkes Regulations
5.1. Progress Reporting (Interim) Sponsor Sponsor must submit clinical trial progress reports to BPOM every 6 months. This report uses the established format (Appendix IX Per-BPOM No. 8/2024).
5.2. Verification and Accountability CRU Sponsor/CRO Close-out visit conducted to ensure data verification, Investigational Product (IP) accountability/destruction. Destruction of remaining Clinical Trial materials must be proven with minutes.
5.3. Final Reporting Sponsor Principal Investigator After Clinical Trial completion, Sponsor must ensure Clinical Trial Report is prepared and provided to regulatory authorities. Final report submitted to BPOM/KE no later than 30 Days from implementation end. Final report must be submitted if study is completed or terminated early.
5.4. INA-CRR Registration (Update) CRU INA-CRC CRU must conduct regular information updates according to clinical trial phase progress on INA-CRR. INA-CRR ensures transparency, accessibility, and accountability.
5.5. Audit and Quality Development BPOM INA-CRC BPOM conducts GCP Inspection before, during, and/or after Clinical Trial to ensure compliance. INA-CRC uses performance data (from Phases II and IV) for CRU Development. Inspection results can be technical development or administrative sanctions.
5.6. National Data Integration INA-CRC Kemenkes INA-CRR data from entire cycle (including performance metrics and PPUK status) must be integrated with National Health Information System (SIKN). Data used for data and evidence-based policy in Health Transformation.
