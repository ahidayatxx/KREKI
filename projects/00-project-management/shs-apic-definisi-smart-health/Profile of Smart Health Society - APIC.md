# **Profile of Smart Health Society of Asosiasi Prakarsa Indonesia Cerdas (APIC)**

## **Context**

This comprehensive profile document serves as a reference for understanding the Smart Health Society of the Asosiasi Prakarsa Indonesia Cerdas (APIC), an organization that promotes and facilitates comprehensive socio-technical healthcare transformation approaches in Indonesia. The document provides detailed information about the organization's mission, theoretical framework, programs, and initiatives to help stakeholders understand its role in advancing evidence-based digital health transformation through systematic implementation science principles and continuous evaluation frameworks. This profile serves as a valuable resource for those collaborating with or seeking to partner with the Smart Health Society of APIC, as well as for those interested in Indonesia's digital health ecosystem transformation.

## **Organization Information**

### **About APIC (Parent Organization)**

Asosiasi Prakarsa Indonesia Cerdas (APIC) is an Indonesian organization formed to meet the need for collaboration among various stakeholders in the country. APIC was officially declared on July 21, 2016, in Surabaya, emerging from the Forum Smart Indonesia Initiatives. The organization aims to bring together diverse stakeholders including government, businesses, industries, academics, community activists, social activists, media, NGOs, and the general public to advance Indonesia's smart initiatives.

APIC was established to support the goal of creating a smart Indonesian society, aligning with the national objective stated in the 1945 Constitution to "educate the life of the nation." The association focuses on developing models for Smart Cities, particularly the Smart City Garuda model, as a reference for the development of smart districts, cities, and villages across Indonesia.

### **Smart Health Society (SHS-APIC): Operational Definition**

**The Smart Health Society (SHS-APIC) promotes and facilitates comprehensive socio-technical healthcare transformation approaches that integrate evidence-based digital technologies with organizational, social, and human factors to achieve equitable, safe, effective, and sustainable health outcomes in Indonesia through systematic implementation science principles, collaborative stakeholder engagement, and continuous evaluation frameworks.**

SHS-APIC is a specialized division within APIC that focuses specifically on smart health initiatives and digital transformation in Indonesia's healthcare sector, handling the development of health services with a focus on technology applications in healthcare. SHS-APIC was formally established through SK No. 183/SK/APIC/3/2025 (Decree No. 183/SK/APIC/3/2025) to serve as a strategic bridge between technological innovation, health policy, and field implementation, grounded in established socio-technical systems theory and implementation science principles (Sittig & Singh, 2010; Damschroder et al., 2024).

## **Theoretical Foundation and Approach**

SHS-APIC's approach is grounded in established socio-technical systems theory, recognizing that successful healthcare transformation requires the integrated optimization of both technical systems (hardware, software, data) and social systems (people, processes, organizations, policies) within Indonesia's specific environmental contexts (Ash et al., 2024). This theoretical foundation draws from Sittig and Singh's seminal eight-dimensional socio-technical model, emphasizing that technology alone cannot improve health outcomes without corresponding attention to human factors, workflow integration, organizational culture, and implementation strategies tailored to Indonesian healthcare settings (Carayon et al., 2024).

The organization's framework incorporates implementation science principles from the Consolidated Framework for Implementation Research (CFIR), ensuring that Smart Health initiatives are designed with attention to intervention characteristics, outer setting factors, inner setting dynamics, individual characteristics, and implementation processes specifically adapted to Indonesia's diverse geographic, cultural, and resource conditions (Damschroder et al., 2024).

## **Mission and Vision**

### **Mission**

To transform healthcare services in Indonesia toward evidence-based, equitable smart health systems by leveraging socio-technical integration approaches that balance digital technologies with human factors, organizational readiness, and implementation science principles to accelerate the adoption and effective implementation of smart health ecosystems across Indonesia's diverse healthcare landscape.

### **Vision**

To establish a comprehensive socio-technical health ecosystem in Indonesia that enhances healthcare delivery, accessibility, and outcomes through digital innovation, multi-stakeholder collaboration, and systematic evaluation frameworks, aligning with Indonesia's Digital Health Transformation Strategy 2025-2029 while addressing health equity and sustainable development goals.

### **Main Tasks**

As outlined in the first plenary meeting on May 4, 2025, SHS-APIC's main task is to accelerate the adoption and implementation of smart health in Indonesia through evidence-based socio-technical approaches, focusing on six core dimensions: socio-technical integration, human-centered design and digital equity, organizational readiness, evidence-based evaluation, health equity integration, and sustainable innovation ecosystem development.

### **Detailed Dimension Integration**

#### **1\. Socio-Technical Integration and Interoperability**

*Primary: Technology & Sustainability | Secondary: Ecosystem & Innovation \+ Security & Privacy*

**Integration Approach:** Technology & Sustainability leads technical standardization (FHIR HL7, SATUSEHAT) while Ecosystem & Innovation ensures social system alignment through Living Labs, and Security & Privacy addresses socio-technical privacy considerations (Kumar & Martinez, 2024).

#### **2\. Human-Centered Design and Digital Equity**

*Primary: Service & Delivery | Secondary: Security & Privacy*

**Integration Approach:** Service & Delivery leads user experience design and accessibility across Indonesia's diverse populations, while Security & Privacy ensures ethical data use and privacy-preserving design for vulnerable populations (Tiase et al., 2024).

#### **3\. Organizational Readiness and Change Management**

*Primary: Policy & Governance | Secondary: Service & Delivery*

**Integration Approach:** Policy & Governance develops readiness assessment frameworks and change management policies, while Service & Delivery implements organizational change strategies in service delivery contexts (Chen et al., 2024).

#### **4\. Evidence-Based Evaluation and Safety Assurance**

*Cross-Cutting: All Working Groups*

**Integration Approach:** Each working group integrates systematic evaluation within their domain expertise, with coordinated health technology assessment protocols and safety monitoring across all initiatives (Miller et al., 2024).

#### **5\. Health Equity and Social Justice Integration**

*Primary: Service & Delivery | Secondary: Policy & Governance*

**Integration Approach:** Service & Delivery leads equity-centered service design and community engagement, while Policy & Governance develops equity impact assessment policies and regulatory frameworks (Naccarella et al., 2024).

#### **6\. Sustainable Innovation Ecosystem Development**

*Primary: Ecosystem & Innovation | Secondary: Technology & Sustainability*

**Integration Approach:** Ecosystem & Innovation leads Living Lab development and multi-stakeholder collaboration, while Technology & Sustainability ensures long-term technical and financial sustainability (Wolff et al., 2024).

## **Core Programs and Initiatives**

### **SATUSEHAT Acceleration Through Socio-Technical Approach**

SHS-APIC's primary focus is accelerating SATUSEHAT adoption using comprehensive socio-technical strategies rather than purely technological implementation. Current implementation data (May 2025\) shows:

* 49,855 (41.94%) of target 118,863 health facilities registered on SATUSEHAT portal  
* 48,215 (40.56%) have integrated and received production API  
* Only 36,515 (30.72%) are actively connected and sending data

**Socio-Technical Intervention Strategy:**

* **Technical Dimension**: FHIR HL7 standards implementation and API integration  
* **Social Dimension**: Staff training, change management, and organizational readiness assessment  
* **Implementation Science**: Phased rollout with continuous monitoring and adaptation  
* **Target**: Increase active connection rate from 30.72% to 65% within two years

### **Digital Health Transformation Through Evidence-Based Implementation**

SHS-APIC advocates for systematic digital transformation using implementation science principles:

* **IoT in Healthcare**: Evidence-based development of IoT applications with human-centered design principles

* **AI in Healthcare**: Comprehensive policy framework development for ethical AI implementation with bias detection protocols
