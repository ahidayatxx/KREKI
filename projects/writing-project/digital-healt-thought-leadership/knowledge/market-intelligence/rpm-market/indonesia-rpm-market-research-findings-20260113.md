## Executive Summary

Indonesia's Remote Patient Monitoring (RPM) market represents a substantial and rapidly expanding opportunity within the broader Southeast Asian healthcare technology ecosystem. The Indonesian connected healthcare market reached USD 0.89 billion in 2025 and is forecasted to climb to USD 3.09 billion by 2030, representing a compound annual growth rate of 28.13%. The RPM segment specifically is growing at 29.6% CAGR, exceeding overall market growth.

Key drivers include:
- Rising chronic disease prevalence (20M diabetes patients, 34.1% hypertension in adults)
- Expanding smartphone and internet penetration (74.6% of population)
- Mandatory SATUSEHAT national health data platform integration (FHIR R4 requirement)
- Government commitment to digital health transformation

## Chronic Disease Burden

### Diabetes
- Prevalence increased from 5.7% (2007) → 6.9% (2013) → 10.9% (2018) → 11.7% (2023)
- Only 32% achieving HbA1c target <7% (suboptimal control)
- 74% of diabetes costs used for managing complications (prevention opportunity)
- Out-of-pocket costs: 9-49% of minimum regional wages

### Hypertension
- National prevalence: 34.1% of adults
- East Java Province: 13.5% (varies significantly by district)
- Gender difference: Women 44.5%, Men 35.9%
- Socioeconomic factor: Poorest have 12.78x greater odds vs. richest

### Cardiovascular Disease
- Deaths: 765,660 in 2021
- Stroke: 90% increase from 1990 (799K → 1,521K cases)
- Heart failure: 25.6% readmission rate, 15% mortality within 3 months
- Peripheral artery disease: Increasing in all provinces

### Hospital Readmissions
- National rate: 5.61% (vs. 1% ideal)
- Financial impact: IDR 3.4M out-of-pocket for stroke patients
- Prevention opportunity: RPM could reduce readmissions by 30%

## Regulatory Environment

### SATUSEHAT Platform (Mandatory Integration)
- **Requirement:** All healthcare facilities must integrate by December 2025
- **Standard:** Health Level 7 Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources (FHIR R4)
- **Current Status:** 92% of hospitals connected to SATUSEHAT
- **Security:** Collaboration with National Cyber and Crypto Agency

### Electronic Medical Records Mandate
- **Regulation:** Minister of Health Regulation No. 24 of 2022
- **Deadline:** December 31, 2023 for digitization transition
- **Status:** 96% of hospitals have implemented EMR systems
- **Quality:** Only small proportion regularly submit comprehensive patient data

### Personal Data Protection Law
- **Law:** Law No. 27 of 2022
- **Compliance Deadline:** October 17, 2024
- **Requirements:** 72-hour breach notification, data subject rights
- **Impact:** Significant compliance obligations for RPM systems

### Omnibus Health Law & Government Regulation 28
- **Legal Basis:** Explicit recognition of telehealth/telemedicine
- **AI Authorization:** Information technology can include robotics and AI
- **Facility Types:** Hospitals, health centers, clinics, independent practices, labs, pharmacies

### BPJS Reimbursement
- **Status:** Uncertain reimbursement policies for RPM
- **Impact:** -1.9% CAGR on market growth (negative impact)
- **Pilot Programs:** Some reimbursement pilots for chronic-care remote monitoring kits
- **Financing:** Capitation payments, case-based groups, non-capitation reimbursements
