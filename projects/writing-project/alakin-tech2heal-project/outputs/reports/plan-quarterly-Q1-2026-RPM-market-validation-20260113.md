## Primary Deliverable: Indonesia RPM Market Validation

### Objective

Validate market demand and business opportunity for Tech2Heal's Alakin RPM platform in Indonesia across three dimensions: (1) market size and growth trajectory for chronic disease remote monitoring, (2) regulatory and reimbursement landscape affecting RPM deployment, and (3) hospital adoption readiness and target segment prioritization.

### Scope

**Geographic:** Indonesia (initial focus: Java-Bali urban centers with expansion planning for Sumatra and Kalimantan)

**Clinical Focus:** Type 2 Diabetes (20 million patients, 11.7% prevalence), Hypertension (34.1% adult prevalence), Heart Failure (25.6% readmission rate)

**Target Segments:**
- Tier 1: BPJS-affiliated hospitals with high readmission rates (readmission rate 5.61% vs. 1% ideal)
- Tier 1: Private hospital groups with digital transformation focus (Siloam, Hermina, Pondok Indah, Asia Medika)
- Tier 2: Academic medical centers with research interest (Universitas Indonesia Hospital, Cipto Mangunkusumo)

**Time Horizon:** 2025-2030 market projections with near-term pilot implementation (Q2 2026)

### Methodology

**Research Approach:**

1. **Literature Review:** Indonesia digital health market reports, Ministry of Health policy documents, BPJS chronic disease management data, WHO/World Bank assessments, industry analyses (McKinsey Health, Deloitte, Frost & Sullivan, Mordor Intelligence)

2. **Competitive Intelligence:** Existing RPM providers in Indonesia (Halodoc, Alodokter, KlikDokter, SehatQ, Good Doctor), international vendors with Indonesia presence (Kakao Healthcare, SPACE Singapore, InterSystems), pricing models and value propositions

3. **Regulatory Analysis:** SATUSEHAT FHIR R4 integration requirements, BPJS reimbursement policies for remote monitoring, Ministry of Health Omnibus Health Law and Government Regulation 28 provisions, Personal Data Protection Law (Law No. 27 of 2022) compliance requirements

4. **Target Segment Validation:** Hospital IT infrastructure readiness (92% connected to SATUSEHAT), clinician acceptance factors, patient smartphone/internet access (74.6% internet penetration), cultural considerations in remote care adoption

**Data Sources:**

- Ministry of Health Indonesia: SATUSEHAT platform documentation, digital health transformation strategy, health information system standards
- BPJS Kesehatan: Chronic disease management expenditure, readmission rate data (561 per 10,000 cases), capitation payment structure
- World Health Organization: Indonesia non-communicable disease country profiles, hypertension and diabetes surveillance
- Research Publications: PubMed studies on Indonesia chronic disease prevalence (diabetes 11.7%, hypertension 34.1%), cardiovascular disease burden (765,660 deaths in 2021), hospital readmission rates
- Industry Reports: Mordor Intelligence Indonesia Connected Healthcare Market (USD 0.89B to USD 3.09B by 2030, 28.13% CAGR), Markets and Markets Global RPM Market (USD 27.7B to USD 56.9B by 2030, 12.7% CAGR)
- Academic Studies: AI-powered heart failure prediction research (Universitas Indonesia), telemedicine adoption studies during COVID-19 (600% surge)

**Analysis Framework:**

- **SATUSEHAT Alignment Filter:** All RPM solutions must demonstrate FHIR R4 compatibility and integration capability with national health information exchange (mandated by December 2025)
- **BPJS Value-Based Care Filter:** Focus on readmission reduction metrics, HbA1c improvement targets, medication adherence enhancement aligned with BPJS quality reporting requirements
- **Tech2Heal Alakin Platform Mapping:** AI-powered care automation, no-code care program development, conversational AI patient app, SATUSEHAT interoperability as key differentiator versus competitors
- **ICP Segment Validation:** Prioritize BPJS hospitals (25% SATUSEHAT readiness weight), private hospital groups (competitive differentiation imperative), academic medical centers (research enablement)

## Appendix: RPM Market Research Summary Findings

### Key Market Statistics

**Market Size & Growth:**
- Indonesia connected healthcare market: USD 0.89 billion (2025) → USD 3.09 billion (2030)
- Compound annual growth rate: 28.13% (overall), 29.6% (RPM segment specifically)
- RPM growing faster than overall market, indicating strong demand trajectory

**Chronic Disease Prevalence (Addressable Population):**
- Diabetes: 11.7% prevalence = 20 million Indonesians (increased from 5.7% in 2007)
- Hypertension: 34.1% of adult population (significant geographic variation)
- Cardiovascular disease: 765,660 deaths in 2021 (leading cause of mortality)
- Only 32% of diabetes patients achieving HbA1c target (suboptimal control = RPM opportunity)

**Hospital Readmission Rates (Pain Point):**
- National readmission rate: 5.61% (vs. 1% ideal benchmark)
- Heart failure readmission: 25.6% (highest readmission risk population)
- Out-of-pocket costs: IDR 3.4M for stroke, IDR 632K for heart disease (9-49% of minimum wages)

**Digital Infrastructure Readiness:**
- Internet penetration: 74.6% of population (212 million users)
- Mobile connections: 356 million (125% of population = multiple devices per person)
- SATUSEHAT connected: 92% of hospitals
- 5G deployment: 15-20ms latency (sufficient for real-time RPM)

**Regulatory Environment:**
- SATUSEHAT FHIR R4 mandate: December 2025 (mandatory integration)
- Personal Data Protection Law: October 2024 compliance deadline
- BPJS reimbursement uncertainty: -1.9% CAGR market impact
