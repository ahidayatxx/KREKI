# **GCP Training Development Framework**

### **Phase 1: Foundational Planning and Design**

This initial phase is critical for establishing the project's scope, objectives, and framework.

**Step 1: Define Target Audience and Learning Objectives**

* **Audience Analysis:** Identify the primary audience as "Investigator Site Personnel"3. This includes Principal Investigators, Sub-Investigators4, Clinical Research Coordinators (CRCs), site pharmacists, and other key staff. The content will need to be relevant to their varying roles and responsibilities.  
* **Learning Objectives:** For each module outlined in the "GCP Training Program: Self-Attestation of TransCelerate Minimum Criteria" document55, establish clear, measurable learning objectives. For example:  
  * **Module 1:** Upon completion, participants will be able to *list* the key principles of ICH GCP and *describe* the ethical foundations of clinical research6666.  
  * **Module 3:** Participants will be able to *explain* the process for submitting documents to an IRB/IEC and *differentiate* between a standard protocol deviation and one requiring immediate action7777.  
  * **Module 4:** Participants will be able to *identify* the criteria for a Serious Adverse Event (SAE) 8888 and *describe* the reporting timelines to the Sponsor and Ethics Committee in Indonesia99.

**Step 2: Map Core Content to Source Documents**

* This is the most crucial step for ensuring compliance and accuracy. The entire training program must be built upon a solid foundation of the primary source materials provided.  
  * **Primary Standard:** The **ICH E6(R3) Guideline** will be the backbone of the training content10101010.  
  * **Indonesian Regulatory Standard:** The **BPOM Regulation No. 8 of 2024** and its appendix on *Cara Uji Klinik yang Baik (CUKB)* will be integrated throughout, especially for country-specific requirements11111111111111.  
  * **Global Best Practices:** The **WHO "Guidance for best practices for clinical trials"** will be used to enrich the training with broader ethical perspectives, a focus on under-represented populations, and the importance of the clinical trial ecosystem121212121212121212.

**Step 3: Instructional Design**

* **Format:** Confirm the 2-day intensive format (16 hours) as specified in the training schedule13.  
* **Methodology:** Design a blended learning experience to maximize engagement and knowledge retention.  
  * **Didactic Presentations:** For foundational concepts like the ICH GCP principles 14 and regulatory definitions15.  
  * **Case Studies:** Develop practical scenarios based on real-world challenges (e.g., managing an SAE, handling a protocol deviation, consenting a vulnerable participant).  
  * **Interactive Discussions:** Facilitate Q\&A sessions and group discussions to explore nuances.  
  * **Group Activities:** Design small group exercises, for example, reviewing a sample informed consent form or creating a delegation log.  
  * **Assessment:** Conclude with a final knowledge assessment and the self-attestation process16.

**Phase 2: Content Development**

In this phase, the detailed training materials are created based on the design from Phase 1\.

**Step 4: Develop Core Module Content (Based on ICH E6(R3))**

* Systematically build out each of the seven modules from the training overview17.  
  * **Module 1: GCP Fundamentals:** Cover the 11 main principles of ICH GCP 18 and define key roles like Sponsor, Investigator, and IRB/IEC191919191919191919.  
  * **Module 2: Investigator Responsibilities:** Detail qualifications 20, resource management 21, delegation of activities 22, and oversight responsibilities23.  
  * **Module 3: Protocol and Regulatory Compliance:** Outline procedures for IRB/IEC communication, ensuring protocol adherence, including deviation management, and trial management processes like premature termination or suspension.  
  * **Module 4: Participant Care and Safety:** Explain the definition of an SAE and SUSAR 24242424 and the investigator's responsibility for safety reporting to the sponsor25252525.  
  * **Module 5: Informed Consent:** Detail the 22 required elements of informed consent information 262626262626262626 and specific procedures for vulnerable populations 27272727, emergency situations 28, and non-literate participants29.  
  * **Module 7: Data Management and Documentation:** Explain the principles of ALCOA-C for source records 30 and the requirements for essential records as listed in Appendix C of ICH E6(R3)31.

**Step 5: Integrate Indonesian Regulations (CUKB/BPOM)**

* Supplement the core ICH content with specific requirements from **BPOM Regulation No. 8 of 2024**. This is not an optional add-on but an essential integration for training delivered in Indonesia32.  
* Key integrations include:  
  * **PPUK (Persetujuan Pelaksanaan Uji Klinik):** Explain that in Indonesia, clinical trials require a PPUK from BPOM before initiation33. Outline the application flow from the provided diagram34.  
  * **Investigator Qualifications:** Emphasize the Indonesian requirement for the Principal Investigator and research assistants to have a CUKB certificate issued within the last 5 years35.  
  * **Reporting Timelines:** Create a clear table showing the specific Indonesian timelines for reporting:  
    * **Investigator to Sponsor:** All SAEs (KTDS) within 24 hours of first knowledge36363636.  
    * **Investigator to Ethics Committee:** All SAEs (KTDS) within 3 calendar days37373737.  
    * **Sponsor to BPOM (for specific trials):** Death-related SAEs within 24 hours; other SAEs within 3 calendar days38.  
  * **Multi-center Trials:** Discuss the CUKB guideline that for multi-center trials in Indonesia, the ethical assessment should ideally be done once at the Principal Investigator's institution39.  
  * **Product Categories:** Use the BPOM classification (Categories I-V) to explain the status of investigational products in the PPUK application form40.

**Step 6: Create Assessment and Certification Materials**

* **Knowledge Assessment:** Develop a 40-50 question multiple-choice and scenario-based exam that covers all seven modules, with specific questions on BPOM regulations. The questions must be mapped to the learning objectives.  
* **Certificate of Completion:** Design a professional certificate that explicitly states the training is "aligned with the TransCelerate-recognized Minimum Criteria for ICH E6 (R3) GCP Investigator Site Personnel Training" and also covers the principles of Indonesia's *Cara Uji Klinik yang Baik (CUKB)*.

### ---

**Phase 3: Material Production**

This phase involves creating the tangible assets for the training session.

**Step 7: Produce Training Materials**

* **Presentation Deck:** Create a comprehensive slide deck (e.g., in PowerPoint) for the instructors, incorporating visuals, diagrams, and key takeaways for each module.  
* **Participant Handbook:** Compile a handbook for attendees that includes:  
  * The 2-day agenda4141.  
  * Key definitions from ICH E6(R3) and BPOM regulations.  
  * Summaries of the ICH GCP Principles.  
  * Copies of case studies and space for notes.  
  * A copy of the PPUK application form from the BPOM regulation appendix42.  
* **Handouts:** Prepare separate printouts for group activities and the final assessment.

### ---

**Phase 4: Implementation and Delivery**

This phase focuses on the execution of the training program.

**Step 8: Instructor Preparation (Train-the-Trainer)**

* Select instructors who are not only GCP experts but also have deep knowledge of Indonesian regulations.  
* Conduct a "Train-the-Trainer" session to review the materials, align on delivery style, and ensure consistent messaging.

**Step 9: Conduct the Training Session**

* Deliver the 2-day intensive program following the established schedule434343.  
* Ensure a highly interactive environment, encouraging questions and discussions, particularly around the practical application of CUKB in the local context.

**Step 10: Administer Assessment and Issue Certificates**

* At the end of Day 2, administer the final assessment44.  
* Collect feedback forms on the training content and delivery.  
* Present the certificates of completion to participants who successfully pass the assessment.

### ---

**Phase 5: Evaluation and Continuous Improvement**

A training program must evolve to remain relevant and effective.

**Step 11: Post-Training Evaluation**

* Analyze the assessment scores and participant feedback to identify areas of strength and opportunities for improvement in the training content and delivery.  
* Consider a follow-up survey 3-6 months post-training to assess knowledge retention and on-the-job application.

**Step 12: Maintain and Update the Program**

* Establish a process for annual review of the training materials.  
* Update the content immediately following any significant changes to ICH E6, BPOM regulations, or other relevant guidelines to ensure the program remains current, compliant, and continues to meet the TransCelerate criteria. The recent finalization of ICH E6(R3) in January 2025 is a perfect example of why this is necessary45454545.
