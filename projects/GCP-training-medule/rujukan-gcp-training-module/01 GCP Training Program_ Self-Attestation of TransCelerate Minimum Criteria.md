**GCP Training Program: Self-Attestation  of TransCelerate Minimum Criteria** 

**Program Overview** 

This training program fulfills TransCelerate's Minimum Criteria for ICH E6 (R3) GCP  Investigator Site Personnel Training, enabling mutual recognition across organizations in  clinical research. 

**Training Modules** 

**Module 1: GCP Fundamentals** 

● **Introduction to GCP** 

○ Definition and importance of Good Clinical Practice 

○ Key terminology: Investigator, Investigator Site, Service Provider 

○ Sub-Investigator roles and responsibilities 

○ Understanding Sponsor functions and obligations 

● **ICH GCP Principles** 

○ Comprehensive review of the 42 ICH GCP Principles 

○ Ethical foundations of clinical research 

○ Practical application in trial settings 

**Module 2: Investigator Responsibilities** 

● **Qualification Requirements** 

○ Education, training, and experience documentation 

○ Demonstrating competence 

○ Investigational product knowledge 

● **Resource Management** 

○ Participant recruitment strategies 

○ Time management for trial conduct 

○ Staff qualification assessment  
○ Facility adequacy evaluation 

● **Delegation and Oversight** 

○ Task delegation procedures 

○ Supervision of delegated activities 

○ Documentation of qualification and delegation ○ Accommodating monitoring and auditing activities 

**Module 3: Protocol and Regulatory Compliance** 

● **IRB/IEC Communication** 

○ Submission responsibilities 

○ Documentation requirements 

○ Approval process 

○ Ongoing reporting obligations 

● **Protocol Adherence** 

○ Documentation of protocol acceptance 

○ Deviation management procedures 

○ Emergency deviation handling 

● **Trial Management** 

○ Premature termination procedures 

○ Suspension protocols 

○ Participant communication during trial changes **Module 4: Participant Care and Safety** 

● **Medical Care Standards** 

○ Healthcare professional involvement 

○ Ongoing medical support 

○ Communication with primary physicians 

● **Safety Reporting** 

○ Adverse event identification and documentation ○ Reporting timelines and procedures 

○ SAE and SUSAR handling 

○ Follow-up requirements 

**Module 5: Informed Consent Process** 

● **Consent Documentation**  
○ IRB/IEC approval requirements 

○ Participant information requirements 

○ Signature and dating protocols 

● **Special Populations** 

○ Minors and incapacitated individuals 

○ Emergency situations 

○ Non-literate participants 

● **Ongoing Consent** 

○ Re-consent procedures 

○ New information communication 

**Module 6: Clinical Trial Operations** 

● **Study Conclusion Procedures** 

○ Managing participant withdrawals 

○ End-of-trial activities 

○ Results communication 

● **Investigational Product Management** 

○ Storage and handling 

○ Inventory control 

○ Participant instructions 

○ Remote administration considerations 

● **Randomization and Blinding** 

○ Implementation procedures 

○ Unblinding protocols 

○ Documentation requirements 

**Module 7: Data Management and Documentation** 

● **Records Management** 

○ Source documentation standards 

○ Data integrity assurance 

○ Privacy and confidentiality protection 

○ Essential documents maintenance 

● **Data Governance** 

○ Data life cycle management 

○ Computerized system requirements 

○ Blinding preservation in data handling 

● **Reporting Requirements**  
○ Progress reporting 

○ Final report preparation 

○ Regulatory submissions 

**Assessment and Certification** 

● Knowledge assessment covering all modules 

● Case studies demonstrating practical application 

● Self-attestation documentation 

● Certificate of completion aligned with TransCelerate requirements **Program Benefits** 

● Mutual recognition across TransCelerate member companies 

● Standardized training aligned with ICH E6 (R3) 

● Reduced redundant training requirements 

● Current best practices in clinical research conduct 

This comprehensive training program satisfies all TransCelerate minimum criteria while  providing practical knowledge for clinical investigators and site personnel.

Addendum: Compliance with Indonesian National Regulations 

The core content of this GCP training program fulfills TransCelerate's Minimum Criteria based  on the ICH Harmonised Guideline for Good Clinical Practice E6(R3). 

In addition to these international standards, for all training delivered to personnel involved in  clinical trials conducted in Indonesia, the program content will be supplemented to ensure  comprehensive understanding and adherence to the prevailing national regulations and  guidelines set forth by the Indonesian National Agency of Drug and Food Control (BPOM).  This includes a thorough understanding of the principles and application of Cara Uji Klinik  yang Baik (CUKB), ensuring all clinical trial activities align with both international GCP  standards and specific Indonesian regulatory expectations.
