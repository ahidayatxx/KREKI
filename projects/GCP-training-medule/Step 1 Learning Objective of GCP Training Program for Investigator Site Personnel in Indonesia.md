### **Comprehensive Learning Objectives Developed for GCP Training Program for Investigator Site Personnel in Indonesia**

**A detailed set of learning objectives has been formulated for a Good Clinical Practice (GCP) training program tailored for investigator site personnel in Indonesia. This framework is designed to meet the TransCelerate-recognized Minimum Criteria for ICH E6 (R3) GCP training while integrating specific national regulations from the Indonesian National Agency of Drug and Food Control (BPOM).**

The primary audience for this training is "Investigator Site Personnel," a group that includes Principal Investigators, Sub-Investigators, Clinical Research Coordinators (CRCs), site pharmacists, and other essential staff involved in clinical trials . The curriculum is structured to be relevant to the varied roles and responsibilities within a clinical research team.

The learning objectives are mapped to the seven-module structure outlined in the "GCP Training Program: Self-Attestation of TransCelerate Minimum Criteria" document and are informed by the core principles of the ICH E6(R3) Guideline, BPOM Regulation No. 8 of 2024 (including the CUKB), and the WHO's "Guidance for best practices for clinical trials" .

Here are the detailed learning objectives for each module:

Module 1: GCP Fundamentals  
Upon completion of this module, participants will be able to:

* List the 11 main principles of ICH GCP .  
* Describe the ethical foundations of clinical research, drawing from the Declaration of Helsinki .  
* Define the roles and responsibilities of the Sponsor, Investigator, and the Institutional Review Board/Independent Ethics Committee (IRB/IEC) .  
* Explain the importance of Good Clinical Practice in ensuring the rights, safety, and well-being of trial participants and the reliability of trial data .

Module 2: Investigator Responsibilities  
Upon completion of this module, participants will be able to:

* Detail the qualifications and training required for an investigator to conduct a clinical trial.  
* Describe the principles of resource management, including adequate staffing and facilities .  
* Explain the process for appropriate delegation of trial-related activities and the importance of oversight .  
* Recognize the Indonesian-specific requirement for Principal Investigators and research assistants to hold a CUKB certificate issued within the last 5 years .

Module 3: Protocol and Regulatory Compliance  
Upon completion of this module, participants will be able to:

* Explain the process for submitting documents to an IRB/IEC for review and approval .  
* Differentiate between a standard protocol deviation and one that requires immediate action to protect participant safety .  
* Describe the process of obtaining *Persetujuan Pelaksanaan Uji Klinik* (PPUK) from BPOM before initiating a clinical trial in Indonesia .  
* Outline the procedure for managing and documenting protocol amendments .

Module 4: Participant Care and Safety  
Upon completion of this module, participants will be able to:

* Identify the criteria for a Serious Adverse Event (SAE) and a Suspected Unexpected Serious Adverse Reaction (SUSAR) .  
* Describe the reporting timelines for SAEs from the investigator to the sponsor and the ethics committee in Indonesia .  
* Articulate the specific Indonesian timelines for reporting SAEs (KTDS): within 24 hours to the Sponsor and within 3 calendar days to the Ethics Committee .  
* Explain the sponsor's responsibility for expedited reporting of death-related SAEs to BPOM within 24 hours and other SAEs within 3 calendar days for specific trials .

Module 5: Informed Consent  
Upon completion of this module, participants will be able to:

* List the 22 required elements of informed consent information as per ICH E6(R3) .  
* Describe the specific procedures for obtaining informed consent from vulnerable populations, in emergency situations, and from non-literate participants .  
* Explain the importance of ensuring the informed consent process is understandable and voluntary for all participants .  
* Discuss the ethical considerations for including under-represented populations in clinical trials, as highlighted by WHO guidance .

Module 6: Clinical Trial Operations  
Upon completion of this module, participants will be able to:

* Describe the procedures for managing the investigational product, including storage, dispensing, and accountability .  
* Explain the principles of randomization and blinding and the procedures for unblinding in emergencies .  
* Outline the procedures for study conclusion, including participant withdrawal and end-of-trial activities .  
* Discuss the CUKB guideline for multi-center trials in Indonesia regarding a single ethical assessment at the Principal Investigator's institution .

Module 7: Data Management and Documentation  
Upon completion of this module, participants will be able to:

* Explain the ALCOA-C principles for maintaining high-quality source records .  
* Identify the essential records for the conduct of a clinical trial as listed in Appendix C of ICH E6(R3) .  
* Describe the requirements for data governance, including data life cycle management and the use of computerized systems .  
* Explain how to use the BPOM classification (Categories I-V) for investigational products in the PPUK application form .
