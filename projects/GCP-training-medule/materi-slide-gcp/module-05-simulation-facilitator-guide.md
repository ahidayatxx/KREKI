# 📚 **Facilitator Guide: Modul 5 Simulation**

## 🎯 **Learning Objectives**

Setelah menyelesaikan simulasi ini, peserta dapat:
1. Mengimplementasikan proses PSP yang efektif untuk populasi beragam tingkat melek huruf
2. Menerapkan teknologi eConsent dengan validasi pemahaman real-time
3. Mengelola isu kepatuhan PSP pada populasi rentan (anak-anak, lansia, disabilitas)
4. Mengatasi hambatan budaya dalam proses persetujuan informasi
5. Mendokumentasikan proses PSP yang memenuhi 22 elemen wajib sesuai regulasi

---

## 🎬 **Step-by-Step Facilitation**

### **Phase 1: Introduction (20 menit)**

**Opening Script:**
> "Selamat pagi para peneliti klinis Indonesia. Hari ini kita akan menghadapi skenario yang mendefinisikan kembali apa arti 'informed consent' di era digital dan AI."
>
> "Anda memimpin penelitian gene therapy pertama di Indonesia dengan PSP system yang canggih: AI-powered comprehension assessment, gamified learning pathways, dan cultural intelligence adaptation. Ini adalah penelitian yang sangat kompleks dengan participants dari berbagai latar belakang: dari pesantren-educated families di Jawa hingga urban teenagers di Surabaya."
>
> "Tantangannya: beberapa families menolak partisipasi karena alasan budaya dan agama, teenagers tidak dilibatkan dalam keputusan tentang tubuh mereka sendiri, dan elderly participants dengan cognitive decline yang families masih ingin ikut sertakan."
>
> "Tujuan kita: menguji kemampuan Anda dalam menyeimbangkan teknologi modern dengan sensitivitas budaya, melindungi vulnerable populations, dan memastikan consent process yang truly informed dalam konteks Indonesia yang sangat beragam."

### **Setup Activities:**
1. Demonstrate PSP technology platform with cultural intelligence features
2. Explain gamified learning system and comprehension assessment tools
3. Distribute cultural context profile packets and family background information
4. Set up virtual counseling simulation platforms

---

### **Phase 2: Crisis Unfolding (30 menit)**

**Progressive Cultural Crisis Strategy:**

#### **First 10 minutes - Initial PSP Validation Failures (09:00-09:15):**
- **AI comprehension alerts** multiple families below threshold
- **Cultural resistance reports** from site coordinators
- **Family conflict notifications** about decision-making authority

**Facilitation Prompts:**
- "Bagaimana Anda menghadapi religious objections terhadap gene therapy?"
- "Kapan cultural adaptation menjadi compromise terhadap standar scientific?"
- "Bagaimana Anda melindungi vulnerable populations sambil menghormati family wishes?"

#### **Next 10 minutes - Escalating Family Conflicts (09:15-09:45):**
- **Religious leader intervention requests** from pesantren community
- **Teenager emotional crises** and autonomy assertion
- **Capacity assessment failures** for elderly participants
- **Family decision-making authority conflicts**

**Building Pressure Techniques:**
- Countdown timer for BPOM inspection preparation
- "Family B teenager locked in room - refusing communication"
- "Community gathering forming outside Bandung clinic"
- "Ethics committee demanding immediate action plan"

#### **Final 10 minutes - Regulatory and Ethical Escalation (09:45-10:15):**
- **BPOM unannounced inspection notification** for tomorrow
- **Media interest** in gene therapy controversy
- **Community health concerns** about genetic manipulation
- **Staff competency questions** about cultural sensitivity

**Crisis Peak Elements:**
- "Regulator questioning validity of digital PSP processes"
- "Families threatening to withdraw en masse"
- "Community leaders requesting meeting with hospital director"
- "Internal team disagreements about cultural accommodation strategies"

---

### **Phase 3: Decision Making (20 menit)**

**Strategic PSP Response Framework:**

#### **Minutes 1-5: Cultural Assessment Strategy**
- Assess cultural barriers across all families
- Determine religious vs. scientific knowledge gaps
- Evaluate family dynamics and decision-making patterns
- Plan culturally appropriate engagement strategies

#### **Minutes 6-12: Technology Integration Planning**
- Determine which PSP elements benefit from technology
- Plan AI-assisted cultural adaptation strategies
- Design hybrid digital-human counseling approaches
- Prepare capacity assessment enhancement tools

#### **Minutes 13-17: Stakeholder Coordination Strategy**
- Plan religious leader engagement approach
- Design family communication strategies
- Prepare staff training for cultural competence
- Coordinate regulatory compliance documentation

#### **Minutes 18-20: Implementation Commitment**
- Choose comprehensive PSP strategy
- Assign responsibility areas for implementation
- Prepare for regulatory inspection
- Plan for continuous consent validation

**Facilitation Support:**
- Use cultural adaptation matrix on whiteboard
- Track family status and intervention strategies
- Record decision rationale for debrief discussion
- Note how participants handle ethical dilemmas

---

### **Phase 4: Debrief (15 menit)**

#### **Part 1: Cultural Competency Analysis (5 menit)**
- "How did you balance scientific requirements with cultural beliefs?"
- "Which strategies worked for overcoming religious objections?"
- "How would you adapt your approach for different cultural contexts?"

#### **Part 2: Technology Integration Reflection (5 menit)**
- "When was human interaction essential despite technology capabilities?"
- "How did digital tools enhance or hinder cultural sensitivity?"
- "What PSP innovations showed the most promise?"

#### **Part 3: Ethical Decision Review (5 menit)**
- "How did you protect vulnerable populations while respecting family wishes?"
- "When is cultural accommodation appropriate vs. over-accommodation?"
- "What ethical frameworks guided your decisions?"

## 🎯 **Key Teaching Points**

### **During Decision Phase:**
1. **Cultural intelligence is essential** for valid informed consent in diverse populations
2. **Technology enhances but doesn't replace human connection** in consent processes
3. **Family-centered approaches require careful balance** with individual autonomy
4. **Capacity assessment must be ongoing** not just one-time determination

### **During Debrief:**
1. **Informed consent requires both comprehension and voluntariness** across cultural contexts
2. **Vulnerable population protection demands specialized approaches** and ongoing assessment
3. **Technology-mediated consent needs new ethical frameworks** and validation methods
4. **Global PSP harmonization requires cultural flexibility** without compromising standards

---

## ⚠️ **Common Participant Responses & Guidance**

### **Response 1: "Follow traditional consent processes and ignore technology"**
**Challenge:** Missed opportunity to enhance comprehension and scalability
**Guiding Question:** "What benefits could technology bring to consent processes? How could we integrate technology while maintaining human connection?"

### **Response 2: "Prioritize family wishes over individual autonomy"**
**Challenge:** May compromise ethical principles and participant rights
**Guiding Question:** "How can we respect cultural values while ensuring genuine voluntary participation? What strategies balance family involvement with individual rights?"

### **Response 3: "Use one-size-fits-all approach for all cultures"**
**Challenge:** Cultural insensitivity and reduced participation rates
**Guiding Question:** "How would cultural adaptation improve understanding and acceptance? What specific cultural considerations should be addressed?"

### **Response 4: "Focus only on regulatory compliance"**
**Challenge:** May miss important cultural and ethical considerations
**Guiding Question:** "How can we go beyond minimum compliance to achieve truly informed consent? What ethical responsibilities extend beyond regulatory requirements?"

### **Response 5: "Assume technology solves all consent problems"**
**Challenge:** Overreliance on technology may overlook human factors
**Guiding Question:** "What aspects of consent require human judgment and interaction? How should technology complement rather than replace human processes?"

## 🔧 **Adaptation Options**

### **For Less Experienced Participants:**
- Reduce number of concurrent family crises (focus on 1-2 families)
- Provide more structured decision frameworks for cultural adaptation
- Extend decision time to 30 menit
- Focus on single cultural context before multiple contexts

### **For Very Experienced Clinical Research Professionals:**
- Add more complex multi-generational family dynamics
- Include political and religious authority interference scenarios
- Add international participant cultural considerations
- Include media crisis and public health responses
- Shorten decision time to 15 menit

### **For Virtual Training:**
- Use breakout rooms for different family counseling sessions
- Simulate virtual religious leader consultations with role-play
- Use shared documents for real-time PSP documentation
- Include AI comprehension assessment simulation with automated feedback

## 📊 **Assessment Criteria**

### **Cultural Competency Skills:**
1. **Cultural Intelligence:** Ability to adapt approaches for diverse cultural contexts
2. **Health Literacy Navigation:** Skill in communicating across education levels
3. **Religious Sensitivity:** Understanding of how religious beliefs impact decisions
4. **Family Dynamics Management:** Ability to navigate family-centered cultures

### **PSP Technology Integration:**
1. **Digital Assessment:** Effective use of technology for comprehension evaluation
2. **Cultural AI Navigation:** Skill in using cultural intelligence tools appropriately
3. **Hybrid Process Design:** Ability to blend digital and human elements effectively
4. **Innovation Implementation:** Capability to integrate new PSP technologies

### **Ethical Decision-Making:**
1. **Autonomy Preservation:** Ensuring genuine voluntary participation
2. **Vulnerable Population Protection:** Appropriate safeguards for at-risk groups
3. **Capacity Assessment:** Ongoing evaluation of decision-making ability
4. **Cultural Balance:** Respecting cultural values while maintaining standards
