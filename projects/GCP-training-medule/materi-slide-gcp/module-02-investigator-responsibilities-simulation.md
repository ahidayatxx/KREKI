# 🎭 **Simulasi Modul 2: Tanggung Jawab Investigator**
## **Krisis Kepemimpinan Era Digital: Remote Workforce, Multi-Site Management, and Technology Integration**

---

## 📋 **Gambaran Umum Simulasi**

### **Konteks:**
Anda adalah **Principal Investigator (PI)** untuk penelitian cardiovascular device yang inovatif dengan **remote monitoring capabilities** dan **multi-site coordination** yang kompleks. Penelitian ini menghadapi tantangan manajemen sumber daya di era digital.

### **Studi Detail:**
- **Device:** AI-powered ECG monitoring patch untuk deteksi aritmia dini
- **Durasi:** 24 bulan dengan 3-year extension
- **Situs:** 7 rumah sakit di 6 kota besar Indonesia
- **Target Enrollment:** 300 pasien dengan komorbiditas diabetes
- **Teknologi:** Real-time ECG transmission, AI analysis, mobile app integration

### **Inovasi Teknologi:**
- **Remote monitoring platform** dengan cloud-based data storage
- **Virtual follow-up visits** menggunakan telemedicine
- **Digital endpoint assessments** dengan machine learning
- **Decentralized data collection** melalui smartphone apps
- **AI-powered adverse event detection**

---

## 🎯 **Learning Objectives**

Setelah menyelesaikan simulasi ini, peserta mampu:
1. Menerapkan prinsip delegasi dalam tim hybrid (on-site + remote)
2. Mengelola multi-site coordination dengan digital tools
3. Mengidentifikasi dan mengatasi kompetensi gaps yang muncul selama studi
4. Membuat keputusan manajemen sumber daya dalam situasi krisis
5. Menyeimbangkan inovasi teknologi dengan kepatuhan CUKB

## 🚨 **Krisis Scenario: Hari ke-180 Penelitian**

### **Timeline - 08:00 WIB:**

**EMAIL dari Site PI Surabaya:**
```
FROM: Dr. Wijaya, PI Surabaya
TO: Dr. Maya Sari (PI Lead)
SUBJECT: URGENT: Multiple Protocol Deviations & Staff Issues

Dear Dr. Maya,

Critical issues at Surabaya site:
1. 3 patients experienced device errors - data not transmitted
2. My CRC resigned suddenly yesterday (personal reasons)
3. Data Manager (Rizky) cannot access cloud storage for 48 hours
4. Junior Sub-I made inappropriate eligibility decisions
5. Next monitoring visit scheduled for next week

Site at risk of major compliance violations. Need immediate guidance.
```

### **Timeline - 08:30 WIB:**

**WhatsApp Messages (multiple):**

**From Makassar Site:**
"Dr, internet down since yesterday. Cannot upload patient data. 15 patients pending follow-up. Telkom says 2-3 days to fix."

**From Junior PI Palembang:**
"Dr Maya, need urgent help. Patient developed severe arrhythmia last night. Device alerted but I'm not sure if this is expected SAE or device malfunction. Patient panicking."

**From Data Manager Rizky (Bali):**
"So sorry Dr Maya. Laptop crashed with all local backups. Cloud access still not working. Lost 2 weeks of data entries for 3 sites."

### **Timeline - 09:00 WIB:**

**Sponsor Emergency Call Summary:**
```
FDA informed us about similar device issues in US study.
They want immediate safety report from all sites within 72 hours.
Also, we need to demonstrate our remote monitoring backup systems.
If not resolved, this could impact global trial continuation.
```

### **Timeline - 09:30 WIB:**

**Hospital Admin Messages:**
- **Jakarta:** Budget freeze for Q4 - cannot hire replacement staff immediately
- **Surabaya:** IT department reports ransomware attack on hospital systems
- **Medan:** Hospital director questioning study safety after patient complaints
- **Palembang:** Media inquiry about "dangerous cardiac device trial"

## 🎯 **Decision Point 1: Resource Allocation Crisis**

### **Immediate Challenges:**

1. **Staffing Emergency:**
   - Surabaya site without CRC (critical role)
   - Data Manager completely offline
   - Need clinical expertise in multiple sites simultaneously

2. **Technology Failures:**
   - Cloud storage inaccessible
   - Local data lost
   - Internet connectivity issues
   - Device safety concerns

3. **Regulatory Pressure:**
   - FDA safety report due in 72 hours
   - BPOM likely to inspect after media reports
   - Protocol compliance violations

4. **Patient Safety:**
   - Multiple device errors
   - Junior PI handling critical events
   - Patient panic and media attention

### **Resource Constraints:**
- **Budget:** Cannot hire immediately
- **Personnel:** Limited qualified staff available
- **Time:** 72 hours to produce safety report
- **Geography:** Sites spread across 6 time zones effectively

---

## 🤔 **Critical Decision Questions**

### **For PI Leadership:**

1. **Prioritization Framework:**
   - Patient safety vs regulatory compliance vs data integrity vs staff retention?
   - Which site gets your immediate attention?
   - How to allocate your limited time across multiple crises?

2. **Delegation Strategy:**
   - Can you safely delegate crisis management?
   - Who has the competence to handle which aspects?
   - How to supervise remote emergency responses?

3. **Technology Response:**
   - Continue with digital systems or revert to manual?
   - How to ensure data integrity during system failures?
   - Backup communication plans?

4. **Stakeholder Communication:**
   - How to inform patients without causing panic?
   - What to tell FDA within 72 hours?
   - How to manage media inquiries?

5. **Long-term Strategy:**
   - Is this model sustainable?
   - What changes needed for future crisis prevention?
   - How to balance innovation with reliability?

### **For CRC Perspective:**

1. **Operational Continuity:**
   - How to maintain patient care with staff shortages?
   - Backup procedures for data collection?
   - Cross-training across sites?

2. **Documentation Challenges:**
   - How to document emergency deviations properly?
   - When to use manual vs electronic systems?
   - Timeline for backdating records?

### **For Site Management:**

1. **Local Problem Solving:**
   - What can be solved locally vs requires PI intervention?
   - When to escalate issues?
   - How to maintain staff morale during crisis?

## 📊 **Assessment Rubric: Investigator Competencies**

### **Resource Management:**
| **Excellent** | **Good** | **Basic** | **Needs Improvement** |
|---------------|----------|-----------|----------------------|
| Demonstrates strategic resource allocation across multiple sites and time zones | Manages resources effectively for immediate priorities | Basic resource allocation for single site | Poor resource planning, ignores constraints |

### **Delegation & Supervision:**
| **Excellent** | **Good** | **Basic** | **Needs Improvement** |
|---------------|----------|-----------|----------------------|
| Effective delegation with appropriate supervision, documentation, and contingency planning | Appropriate delegation with basic supervision | Minimal delegation, micromanagement | Poor delegation or no delegation when appropriate |

### **Crisis Leadership:**
| **Excellent** | **Good** | **Basic** | **Needs Improvement** |
|---------------|----------|-----------|----------------------|
| Calm, decisive leadership during multiple simultaneous crises, clear communication | Manages crisis effectively with reasonable decisions | Basic crisis response, may miss key issues | Panicked response or indecisive during crisis |

### **Digital Competence:**
| **Excellent** | **Good** | **Basic** | **Needs Improvement** |
|---------------|----------|-----------|----------------------|
| Understands digital limitations, implements robust backup systems | Basic understanding of digital tools and limitations | Minimal digital literacy, resists technology | No understanding of digital trial requirements |

## 💡 **Key Learning Takeaways**

### **Modern Investigator Responsibilities:**
1. **Technology is both solution and risk** - Digital tools enable innovation but create new failure modes
2. **Remote work requires different supervision** - Traditional onsite management doesn't translate to virtual teams
3. **Crisis management is part of core competency** - Modern PIs need emergency response skills
4. **Resource planning must include digital contingencies** - Backup systems are not optional

### **Leadership in Clinical Research:**
1. **Communication is most critical during crises** - Clear, transparent communication prevents panic
2. **Team resilience matters more than individual expertise** - Cross-trained teams survive failures
3. **Regulatory relationships require proactive management** - Don't wait for regulators to contact you
4. **Patient safety always comes first** - Even when facing compliance pressure

### **Future of Clinical Trials:**
1. **Hybrid models will become standard** - Mix of digital and traditional methods
2. **Local adaptation is essential** - Global technologies need local customization
3. **Continuous learning is required** - Technology evolves faster than traditional training cycles
4. **Collaboration across sites is survival skill** - No single site can handle complex modern trials alone
