# 📚 **Facilitator Guide: Modul 4 Simulation**

## 🎯 **Learning Objectives**

Setelah menyelesaikan simulasi ini, peserta dapat:
1. Mengidentifikasi dan mengelola safety alerts dari AI monitoring systems
2. Menavigasi conflicts antara digital health predictions dan clinical judgments
3. Mengimplementasikan emergency response protocols untuk digital health trials
4. Mengelola cultural sensitivities dalam teknologi healthcare adoption
5. Mendokumentasikan digital safety events sesuai regulasi

---

## ⏰ **Recommended Timeline**

| **Phase** | **Duration** | **Activity** |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| **Setup** | 15 min | Digital health ecosystem briefing, AI system demonstration |
| **Crisis Unfolding** | 30 min | Multi-patient safety alerts with technology system failures |
| **Decision Making** | 20 menit | Emergency response strategy development and implementation |
| **Debrief** | 25 menit | Digital health safety analysis and cultural competency reflection |

## 🎬 **Step-by-Step Facilitation**

### **Phase 1: Introduction (15 menit)**

**Opening Script:**
> "Selamat pagi tim perawatan penelitian klinik. Hari ini kita akan menghadapi skenario yang akan menjadi standar dalam 5 tahun ke depan: krisis keamanan di era digital health dan AI monitoring."
>
> "Anda adalah CRC untuk penelitian heart failure yang menggunakan sistem monitoring canggih: wearable ECG devices, AI-powered safety predictions, dan telemedicine consultations. Ini adalah penelitian digital therapeutic pertama di Indonesia dengan skala besar."
>
> "Tapi teknologi canggih tidak tanpa risiko. Hari ini, sistem AI mengirim multiple safety alerts secara bersamaan, beberapa pasien tidak dapat dihubungi, dan ada indikasi device malfunction yang mempengaruhi 15 pasien sekaligus."
>
> "Yang paling menantang: ada konflik antara AI predictions dengan kenyataan klinis, tantangan budaya dengan teknologi, dan tekanan regulasi yang ketat. Tujuan kita adalah menguji kemampuan Anda dalam memprioritaskan keselamatan pasien di era digital health."

### **Setup Activities:**
1. Demonstrate AI safety dashboard functionality
2. Explain wearable device capabilities and limitations
3. Distribute role-specific patient information packets
4. Set up multi-site communication simulation systems

---

### **Phase 2: Crisis Unfolding (30 menit)**

**Progressive Digital Health Crisis Strategy:**

#### **First 10 minutes - AI Safety Alerts (08:00-08:15):**
- **Multiple simultaneous AI alerts:** High-risk patient predictions
- **Technology system warnings:** Device connectivity issues
- **Site emergency calls:** Multiple sites reporting different problems

**Facilitation Prompts:**
- "Bagaimana Anda memprioritaskan antara AI predictions dengan laporan klinis dari situs?"
- "Pasien mana yang memerlukan respons paling mendesak?"
- "Bagaimana Anda mengoordinas respons multi-situs secara efektif?"

#### **Next 10 minutes - Technology System Failures (08:15-08:45):**
- **Family resistance to technology:** Cultural barriers to care
- **Device malfunction vendor notification:** Technical system failures
- **Communication system breakdowns:** Telemedicine platform down

**Building Pressure Techniques:**
- Countdown clocks for patient response time windows
- "Patient #087 cardiac arrhythmia detected 45 seconds ago"
- "Device manufacturer reporting 20% failure rate in field"
- "Telemedicine platform ETA for restoration: Unknown"

#### **Final 10 minutes - Escalation Peak (08:45-09:30):**
- **Patient death reported:** Suspected relation to study device
- **Family legal threats:** Potential lawsuit preparation
- **BPOM regulatory alert:** Investigation team deployment
- **Media attention preparation:** Journalist inquiries beginning

**Crisis Peak Elements:**
- "BPOM automatic safety signal triggered - investigation team en route"
- "Family lawyer threatening lawsuit within 2 hours"
- "National media picking up story about 'dangerous clinical trial device'"
- "Device manufacturer requesting immediate recall consideration"

---

### **Phase 3: Decision Making (20 menit)**

**Strategic Emergency Response Framework:**

#### **Minutes 1-5: Rapid Triage**
- Assess all patient safety situations simultaneously
- Determine immediate life-threatening conditions
- Evaluate technology system reliability
- Initiate emergency medical response teams

#### **Minutes 6-12: Response Coordination**
- Deploy medical resources to highest priority cases
- Coordinate technology vendor response
- Initiate family and community engagement
- Begin regulatory compliance documentation

#### **Minutes 13-17: Cultural Engagement**
- Address technology resistance in affected communities
- Engage community leaders and family members
- Adapt communication strategies for different cultural contexts
- Build trust for continued medical care

#### **Minutes 18-20: Strategy Commitment**
- Choose comprehensive response approach
- Assign responsibility areas across team
- Prepare for regulatory investigation
- Plan for media and public communication

**Facilitation Support:**
- Use emergency response matrix on whiteboard
- Track patient status across multiple sites
- Record decision rationale for debrief discussion
- Note how participants handle conflicting priorities

---

### **Phase 4: Debrief (25 menit)**

#### **Part 1: Emergency Response Analysis (8 menit)**
- "Which patient received appropriate care first?"
- "How did you balance AI predictions with clinical judgment?"
- "What communication strategies were most effective with families?"

#### **Part 2: Technology Assessment (7 menit)**
- "When did you trust vs. question AI predictions?"
- "How did you handle technology system failures?"
- "What backup systems were most valuable during crisis?"

#### **Part 3: Cultural Sensitivity Reflection (5 menit)**
- "How did you address cultural barriers to care?"
- "What strategies worked for building trust with skeptical communities?"
- "How would you adapt your approach for different cultural contexts?"

#### **Part 4: Digital Health Future Planning (5 menit)**
- "What new safety protocols are needed for AI-powered monitoring?"
- "How should we validate digital health technologies before implementation?"
- "What role should AI play in future clinical trial safety monitoring?"

## 🎯 **Key Teaching Points**

### **During Decision Phase:**
1. **Patient safety always transcends technology limitations**
2. **Cultural sensitivity is as important as medical competence**
3. **Technology requires human oversight and validation**
4. **Emergency response must be multi-modal and redundant**

### **During Debrief:**
1. **Digital health creates new safety paradigms** that require new thinking
2. **AI is a tool, not a replacement for clinical judgment**
3. **Cultural competency is essential for technology adoption**
4. **Regulatory frameworks need to evolve with digital health innovation**

## ⚠️ **Common Participant Responses & Guidance**

### **Response 1: "Trust the AI predictions completely and respond immediately"**
**Challenge:** AI predictions may be inaccurate or based on faulty data
**Guiding Question:** "How would you validate AI predictions before taking medical action? What if the algorithm is wrong?"

### **Response 2: "Ignore technology and use traditional methods only"**
**Challenge:** Missing potential early warning signs and benefits of digital monitoring
**Guiding Question:** "What valuable information might be lost by ignoring digital monitoring systems? How can we best integrate both approaches?"

### **Response 3: "Force families to accept medical care regardless of cultural beliefs"**
**Challenge:** Cultural insensitivity and potential loss of trust
**Guiding Question:** "How can we respect cultural beliefs while ensuring patient safety? What community engagement strategies might work better?"

### **Response 4: "Focus only on regulatory compliance requirements"**
**Challenge:** Patient safety may be compromised by regulatory focus
**Guiding Question:** "How can we balance regulatory compliance with urgent medical needs? When should patient safety override protocol requirements?"

### **Response 5: "Blame the technology vendor and wait for fixes"**
**Challenge:** Delay in patient care and lack of immediate solutions
**Guiding Question:** "What immediate actions can you take while working with vendors? How can you provide patient care despite technology issues?"

## 🔧 **Adaptation Options**

### **For Less Experienced Participants:**
- Reduce number of simultaneous patient safety alerts
- Provide more structured decision-making frameworks
- Extend decision time to 30 menit
- Focus on single-site scenario before multi-site coordination

### **For Very Experienced Clinical Research Professionals:**
- Add more complex AI algorithm accuracy questions
- Include international regulatory coordination requirements
- Add bioethics committee consultation scenarios
- Include patient advocacy group involvement
- Shorten decision time to 15 menit

### **For Virtual Training:**
- Use breakout rooms for different site teams
- Simulate video calls with family members and community leaders
- Use shared documents for real-time KTDS reporting
- Include AI dashboard simulation with automated alerts
