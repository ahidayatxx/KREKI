## 📖 **Investigator Responsibilities Quick Reference (ICH E6(R3) 4.1-4.12)**

### **Core Responsibilities:**
1. **Conduct study per protocol** - Modify only with proper documentation
2. **Protect subject safety** - Prioritize over all other considerations
3. **Ensure adequate resources** - Staff, facilities, and systems
4. **Maintain qualified staff** - Proper training and supervision
5. **Delegate appropriately** - To qualified personnel with oversight
6. **Obtain informed consent** - Personally ensure proper process
7. **Provide medical care** - To subjects for trial-related issues
8. **Report adverse events** - Timely and accurate reporting

### **Documentation Requirements:**
- **Log Delegasi:** Semua tugas yang didelegasikan
- **Training Records:** Bukti kompetensi staf
- **Protocol Deviations:** Dokumentasi lengkap dengan justifikasi
- **Safety Reports:** KTDS dan SAE reporting
- **Communications:** Semua komunikasi penting

## 🚨 **Crisis Response Framework**

### **Step 1: Immediate Assessment (First 5 minutes)**
- [ ] **Patient Safety Check:** Is anyone in immediate danger?
- [ ] **System Status:** What digital systems are down vs working?
- [ ] **Staff Availability:** Who is present and competent?
- [ ] **Regulatory Deadlines:** What reports are due and when?
- [ ] **Media Alerts:** Any public attention or patient complaints?

### **Step 2: Priority Matrix**
```
URGENT & IMPORTANT (Do Now):
- Patient safety issues
- Regulatory reporting deadlines
- Life-threatening device malfunctions

IMPORTANT & NOT URGENT (Plan):
- Staff replacement planning
- System recovery procedures
- Long-term protocol modifications

URGENT & NOT IMPORTANT (Delegate):
- Routine patient follow-ups
- Regular data entry tasks
- Non-emergency communications
```

### **Step 3: Communication Protocol**
```
PATIENTS → Clear, calm, factual updates
STAFF → Specific instructions and support
SPONSOR → Transparent problem reporting
REGULATOR → Timely compliance notifications
MEDIA → Coordinated official statements only
```

## 📱 **Digital Trial Crisis Management**

### **System Failure Response:**
1. **Immediate Switch:** Manual data collection procedures
2. **Backup Activation:** Paper CRFs, phone follow-ups
3. **Data Recovery:** Assess what can be restored vs lost
4. **Communication:** Inform all sites of manual procedures
5. **Documentation:** Record all system failures and responses

### **Remote Team Challenges:**
- **Verify Staff Competence:** Can remote staff handle emergencies?
- **Backup Communication:** Multiple channels (WhatsApp, phone, email)
- **Site Support:** Mentoring for less experienced sites
- **Documentation:** Remote work requires extra documentation

### **Technology Contingency Planning:**
- **Alternative Data Collection:** Paper forms ready
- **Multiple Communication Channels:** Not dependent on single system
- **Local Data Backups:** Each site maintains local copies
- **Emergency Procedures:** What to do when systems completely fail

## ⚖️ **Regulatory Response Plan**

### **FDA Safety Report (72 hours):**
1. **Gather Information:** All device errors, patient impacts
2. **Assess Severity:** Life-threatening vs. non-serious
3. **Root Cause Analysis:** Technical failures vs. user error
4. **Corrective Actions:** What immediate steps taken
5. **Prevention Plan:** How to prevent recurrence

### **BPOM Notification Requirements:**
- **Immediate:** Life-threatening situations (24 hours)
- **Protocol Deviations:** Within 5 working days
- **System Failures:** As soon as discovered
- **Media Inquiries:** Coordinate with sponsor

### **Documentation Needed:**
- [ ] Device error reports from all sites
- [ ] Patient safety assessments
- [ ] Timeline of events
- [ ] Corrective action plan
- [ ] Impact assessment on data integrity

## 🎯 **Decision-Making Framework**

### **For Each Decision:**
1. **Patient Impact:** Does this protect or risk patient safety?
2. **Regulatory Compliance:** Does this follow CUKB principles?
3. **Data Integrity:** Does this protect or compromise study data?
4. **Feasibility:** Is this actually possible with current resources?
5. **Communication:** How will this be explained to stakeholders?

### **Delegation Decision Tree:**
```
CAN THIS TASK BE DELEGATED?
├─ NO → PI must handle personally
└─ YES → Is staff qualified?
   ├─ NO → PI must handle or train first
   └─ YES → Has staff been trained on this specific task?
      ├─ NO → Train before delegating
      └─ YES → Delegate with proper supervision and documentation
```

## 🔥 **Crisis Communication Strategies**

### **Internal Team:**
- **Clear priorities:** What matters most right now?
- **Specific tasks:** Not just "help out" but exact responsibilities
- **Reporting structure:** Who reports to whom and when
- **Support systems:** Mental health and stress management

### **External Stakeholders:**
- **Unified message:** Consistent information from all sources
- **Designated spokesperson:** Only authorized individuals speak
- **Regular updates:** Prevent speculation with regular communication
- **Empathy and transparency:** Acknowledge concerns honestly

### **Media Management:**
- **No immediate response:** Take time to assess situation
- **Official statements only:** Coordinate with sponsor
- **Patient privacy:** Never share identifiable patient information
- **Focus on solutions:** What's being done to address problems

## 💡 **Leadership Reminders**

### **Under Pressure:**
1. **Breathe:** Take 30 seconds before making critical decisions
2. **Prioritize:** Patient safety first, everything else second
3. **Document:** You will be judged on your documentation
4. **Communicate:** Silence creates speculation and fear
5. **Ask for Help:** No one expects you to handle everything alone

### **Decision Quality Indicators:**
✓ **Based on evidence** - Not emotion or pressure
✓ **Considers all stakeholders** - Patients, staff, regulators, sponsor
✓ **Documented rationale** - Clear reasoning for decisions
✓ **Reversible where possible** - Don't close doors unnecessarily
✓ **Professional communication** - Calm, clear, transparent

## 🚀 **Prevention Planning for Future**

### **System Redundancies:**
- **Multiple data collection methods** - Manual + digital
- **Various communication channels** - Phone, WhatsApp, email
- **Backup power sources** - Generators, battery packs
- **Alternative monitoring sites** - Nearby hospitals as backup

### **Staff Cross-Training:**
- **Multiple staff per critical role** - No single points of failure
- **Regular competency assessments** - Verify skills remain current
- **Emergency procedure training** - Practice crisis scenarios
- **Mentorship programs** - Senior staff guide junior staff

### **Protocol Design Improvements:**
- **Built-in contingencies** - Plans for system failures
- **Gradual technology adoption** - Start simple, add complexity
- **Local adaptation requirements** - Consider site capabilities
- **Regular review cycles** - Update procedures as technology evolves
