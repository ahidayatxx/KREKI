## 📖 **22 Mandatory PSP Elements Quick Reference**

### **Basic Study Information:**
1. **Research Purpose** - Clear explanation in lay language
2. **Study Duration** - Time commitment including follow-up
3. **Procedures** - What will happen during participation
4. **Experimental Nature** - Clear distinction from standard care

### **Risk-Benefit Assessment:**
5. **Reasonably Foreseeable Risks** - All potential harms explained
6. **Potential Benefits** - Realistic expectations of benefit
7. **Alternative Procedures** - Standard treatment options
8. **Confidentiality Limits** - What information remains private

### **Voluntary Participation:**
9. **Voluntary Nature** - No coercion explanation
10. **Withdrawal Rights** - How to withdraw without penalty
11. **New Information** - How changes will be communicated
12. **Compensation** - Payment for time and expenses

### **Special Considerations:**
13. **Costs** - Any expenses to participants
14. **Injury Compensation** - What happens if harmed
15. **Contact Information** - Who to call with questions
16. **Rights and Refusal** - Declining participation explained

### **Indonesian Regulatory:**
17. **Ethics Committee Approval** - KEPK review confirmation
18. **BPOM Registration** - Clinical trial authorization
19. **Data Protection** - Personal information security
20. **Sample Storage** - Future use of biological samples

### **Advanced Consent:**
21. **Genetic Information** - Special genetic data considerations
22. **Publication Plans** - How results will be shared

## ⚖️ **Vulnerable Population Protection Framework**

### **Pediatric Participants:**

#### **Assent Requirements:**
- **Age 6-11:** Simple explanation, verbal or picture-based agreement
- **Age 12-17:** Detailed explanation appropriate to maturity level
- **Age 16-17:** Consider mature minor assessment
- **Age 18+:** Adult consent procedures

#### **Parental Permission Process:**
1. **Both Parents Required:** Unless one parent has legal sole custody
2. **Divorced Families:** Documentation of legal custody arrangements
3. **Single Parent:** Proof of sole legal guardianship
4. **Guardians:** Legal documentation of guardianship authority

#### **Child-Friendly Materials:**
- **Picture Books:** Visual explanations for young children
- **Interactive Apps:** Engaging digital consent education
- **Video Demonstrations:** Age-appropriate procedure explanations
- **Child Advocates:** Independent support for child participants

### **Elderly Participants:**

#### **Capacity Assessment Checklist:**
- [ ] **Memory:** Can recall study purpose and procedures
- [ ] **Understanding:** Explains study risks and benefits in own words
- [ ] **Reasoning:** Can explain why participation is beneficial
- [ ] **Expression:** Can clearly state consent or refusal
- [ ] **Consistency:** Decision remains stable over time

#### **Cognitive Impairment Indicators:**
- **Forgetfulness:** Repeatedly asking same questions
- **Confusion:** Difficulty understanding basic concepts
- **Inconsistent Answers:** Contradictory statements about study
- **Family Dependence:** Relying entirely on family for decisions
- **Time Disorientation:** Confusion about study timeline

#### **Surrogate Consent Guidelines:**
1. **Legal Guardian:** Court-appointed guardian when patient lacks capacity
2. **Healthcare Proxy:** Legally designated decision-maker
3. **Family Consensus:** Agreement among legal next-of-kin
4. **Best Interest Standard:** Decision based on patient welfare
5. **Previously Expressed Wishes:** Known patient preferences

### **Cognitively Impaired Participants:**

#### **Capacity Enhancement Strategies:**
- **Extended Discussion Time:** Multiple sessions for understanding
- **Simplified Materials:** Easy-to-read consent documents
- **Visual Aids:** Diagrams and illustrations
- **Teach-Back Method:** Participant explains understanding in own words
- **Family Involvement:** Supportive family participation

#### **Ongoing Capacity Monitoring:**
- **Regular Assessment:** Re-evaluation at critical study points
- **Withdrawal Recognition:** Right to withdraw if capacity changes
- **Advance Planning:** Documentation of preferences
- **Caregiver Training:** Support for ongoing decision-making

## 🔥 **Crisis Response Guidelines**

### **Religious Objection Management:**

#### **Immediate Response Steps:**
1. **Listen Respectfully:** Understand specific religious concerns
2. **Acknowledge Beliefs:** Validate importance of faith in decision-making
3. **Provide Scientific Context:** Explain technology in religiously sensitive terms
4. **Engage Religious Leaders:** Involve respected faith community leaders
5. **Offer Alternatives:** Non-participation options without judgment

#### **Scientific-Religious Bridge Building:**
```
ISLAMIC PERSPECTIVE:
"Mencari obat adalah wajib dalam Islam. Al-Qur'an mengajarkan untuk menyembuhkan."
"Teknologi ini adalah pemberian Allah untuk membantu manusia."
"Tujuannya adalah menyembuhkan, bukan mengubah ciptaan Allah."

CHRISTIAN PERSPECTIVE:
"Kristianitas mengajarkan kasih dan penyembuhan bagi yang menderita."
"Ilmu pengetahuan adalah anugerah untuk mengurangi penderitaan."
"Tuhan memberikan kebijaksanaan kepada manusia untuk mengembangkan obat."
```

### **Family Conflict Resolution:**

#### **Mediation Strategies:**
1. **Neutral Facilitation:** Impartial third-party support
2. **Cultural Mediator:** Community leader involvement
3. **Professional Counseling:** Social worker or psychologist support
4. **Time for Decision:** Allow adequate time for family discussion
5. **Documentation:** Record family consensus process

#### **Conflict Types and Approaches:**
```
PARENT-CHILD CONFLICT:
- Approach: Child autonomy vs. parental authority
- Strategy: Age-appropriate assent process
- Timeline: Allow time for family adjustment

MULTI-GENERATIONAL CONFLICT:
- Approach: Elder wisdom vs. modern medicine
- Strategy: Respect traditional knowledge
- Timeline: Extended family discussion period

SIBLING DISAGREEMENT:
- Approach: Different opinions about participation
- Strategy: Evidence-based family meeting
- Timeline: Professional mediation support
```

### **Technology Resistance Management:**

#### **Gradual Technology Introduction:**
1. **Demonstration First:** Show technology before requesting use
2. **Human Support Available:** Staff assistance always present
3. **Alternative Options:** Traditional methods available as backup
4. **Success Stories:** Examples of technology helping others
5. **Patience and Support:** Allow adequate learning time

#### **Digital Literacy Support:**
```
LOW LITERACY SUPPORT:
- Picture-based explanations
- Voice-over instructions
- Large text and simple navigation
- One-on-one technology tutoring
- Family member assistance

MODERATE LITERACY SUPPORT:
- Interactive tutorials
- Step-by-step guides
- Video demonstrations
- Practice sessions before real use
- Technical support hotlines
```
