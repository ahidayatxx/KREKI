# 📚 **Facilitator Guide: Modul 6 Simulation**

## 🎯 **Learning Objectives**

Setelah menyelesaikan simulasi ini, peserta dapat:
1. Mengelola investigational product supply chain dengan teknologi IoT-enabled smart systems
2. Menerapkan real-time quality monitoring dengan AI-powered compliance automation
3. Mengkoordinasikan multi-site operations dengan digital communication platforms
4. Mengatasi operational disruptions dengan blockchain-based contingency planning
5. Mendokumentasikan operational excellence dengan digital audit trail systems

## 🎬 **Step-by-Step Facilitation**

### **Phase 1: Introduction (15 menit)**

**Opening Script:**
> "Selamat pagi operations professionals. Hari ini kita akan menghadapi skenario yang akan menjadi standar dalam 5 tahun ke depan: krisis operasional di era digital transformation clinical trials."
>
> "Anda memimpin operations untuk multi-site adaptive platform trial pertama di Indonesia dengan sistem operasional canggih: IoT-enabled supply chain, AI-powered quality monitoring, blockchain documentation, dan drone delivery network."
>
> "Tiba-tiba, solar storm activity menyebabkan nationwide IoT network failure, AI quality system mengalami cascade error, dan multiple sites mengalami supply chain disruptions. Beberapa sites tanpa power, drone delivery gagal, dan BPOM automated compliance system mengirim alerts."
>
> "Tantangannya: mempertahankan patient safety sambil mengelola multiple technology failures, memenuhi regulatory requirements dengan manual procedures, dan mengkoordinasikan emergency response across 10 sites di 6 provinsi yang berbeda."

### **Setup Activities:**
1. Demonstrate digital operations dashboard and monitoring systems
2. Explain IoT-enabled supply chain technology and failure points
3. Assign site-specific operational capability profiles
4. Set up multi-site communication simulation platform

---

### **Phase 2: Crisis Unfolding (30 menit)**

**Progressive Operational Crisis Strategy:**

#### **First 10 minutes - Initial Technology Failures (08:00-08:30):**
- **IoT network outage** across multiple sites
- **AI quality system cascade** blocking essential procedures
- **Central command connectivity** failures requiring satellite communication
- **Site-specific emergencies** (power failures, supply shortages)

**Facilitation Prompts:**
- "How do you prioritize patient needs when technology systems fail?"
- "Which digital systems are essential versus dispensable during emergencies?"
- "How do you coordinate response across geographically distributed sites?"

#### **Next 10 minutes - Escalating Supply Chain Disruptions (08:30-09:30):**
- **Investigational product temperature excursion risks** at critical sites
- **Drone delivery failures** due to weather conditions
- **Manual accountability systems** implementation challenges
- **Blockchain synchronization delays** creating compliance gaps

**Building Pressure Techniques:**
- Real-time countdown for critical patient medication timing
- "Manado site temperature excursion risk - 2 hours until product unusable"
- "BPOM automated compliance system detected multiple violations"
- "Sponsor requesting immediate study status report and resource allocation decisions"

#### **Final 10 minutes - Regulatory and Sponsor Escalation (09:30-10:30):**
- **BPOM compliance alerts** requiring immediate reporting
- **Patient safety critical events** requiring emergency unblinding
- **Study sponsor budget approvals** for emergency contingency funds
- **Technology vendor responses** with extended restoration timelines

**Crisis Peak Elements:**
- "Patient #047 requires urgent medication - no supply available at Manado site"
- "AI system unblinding blocked - manual override requires regulatory notification"
- "Blockchain audit trail gaps creating compliance violations across 6 sites"
- "Sponsor considering temporary study hold due to operational integrity concerns"

---

### **Phase 3: Decision Making (20 menit)**

**Strategic Operations Response Framework:**

#### **Minutes 1-5: Rapid Operational Assessment**
- Assess technology failure impact across all sites
- Determine patient safety-critical needs prioritization
- Evaluate manual procedure capability and resources
- Initiate emergency communication protocols

#### **Minutes 6-12: Resource Allocation Strategy**
- Deploy emergency supplies to highest priority sites
- Coordinate manual override procedures across sites
- Activate backup communication systems
- Plan regulatory compliance documentation strategies

#### **Minutes 13-17: Multi-Site Coordination**
- Implement centralized command with local autonomy balance
- Coordinate manual accountability procedures across sites
- Manage emergency patient care interventions
- Plan technology system recovery phases

#### **Minutes 18-20: Decision Commitment**
- Choose comprehensive operations strategy
- Assign responsibility areas across sites
- Prepare for regulatory notifications and reporting
- Plan for post-crisis system improvements

**Facilitation Support:**
- Use operations decision matrix comparing manual vs. hybrid approaches
- Track site status and resource allocation decisions
- Record technology vs. human judgment decision points
- Note coordination challenges across distributed sites

---

### **Phase 4: Debrief (15 menit)**

#### **Part 1: Operations Strategy Analysis (5 menit)**
- "How did you balance patient safety with operational integrity during technology failures?"
- "Which manual procedures were most effective for maintaining compliance?"
- "When should automated systems be overridden with human judgment?"

#### **Part 2: Multi-Site Coordination Challenges (5 menit)**
- "How did you allocate limited emergency resources across multiple sites?"
- "What communication strategies worked best for coordinating distributed teams?"
- "How should centralized control vs. local autonomy be balanced?"

#### **Part 3: Technology Resilience Planning (5 menit)**
- "What backup systems should be standard for future operations?"
- "How can operational systems be designed for better failure tolerance?"
- "What training is needed for manual-digital hybrid operations?"

---

## 🎯 **Key Teaching Points**

### **During Decision Phase:**
1. **Operational redundancy is essential** - Single points of failure unacceptable in critical trial operations
2. **Human judgment must override technology** during patient safety emergencies
3. **Multi-site coordination complexity** requires both centralized control and local autonomy
4. **Regulatory compliance adapts** to technology disruptions but requires careful documentation

### **During Debrief:**
1. **Digital transformation increases efficiency** but creates new failure modes requiring backup planning
2. **Operational excellence requires both technology proficiency and manual competence**
3. **Crisis coordination skills** become more critical with distributed multi-site trials
4. **Technology resilience planning** must be integrated from study design rather than added later

---

## ⚠️ **Common Participant Responses & Guidance**

### **Response 1: "Maintain full digital operations and wait for technology recovery"**
**Challenge:** Patient care delays and potential safety risks
**Guiding Question:** "What patient safety risks occur while waiting for technology recovery? How can manual procedures maintain patient care continuity?"

### **Response 2: "Immediately switch to complete manual operations"**
**Challenge:** Regulatory compliance documentation gaps and resource requirements
**Guiding Question:** "How will you maintain regulatory compliance and documentation integrity with manual procedures? What resources are needed for full manual operations?"

### **Response 3: "Continue operations at well-equipped sites only, pause others"**
**Challenge:** Equity issues and study timeline impacts
**Guiding Question:** "How will you ensure fair access across all sites? What impact will selective site continuation have on study validity?"

### **Response 4: "Prioritize regulatory compliance over patient needs temporarily"**
**Challenge:** Ethical obligations to patient care
**Guiding Question:** "When does regulatory compliance become secondary to patient safety? How can you maintain both simultaneously?"

### **Response 5: "Let each site make independent decisions based on local capabilities"**
**Challenge:** Study consistency and regulatory coordination issues
**Guiding Question:** "How will you maintain study consistency across sites with independent decision-making? What coordination mechanisms are needed?"

## 🔧 **Adaptation Options**

### **For Less Experienced Participants:**
- Reduce number of sites (focus on 3-4 sites instead of 10)
- Provide more structured decision frameworks for operational triage
- Extend decision time to 30 menit
- Focus on single operational challenge rather than multiple cascading failures

### **For Very Experienced Operations Professionals:**
- Add international site coordination challenges with different regulatory requirements
- Include cyberattack scenarios alongside natural disasters
- Add budget constraint considerations and ROI analysis
- Include competitive pressure and timeline acceleration challenges
- Shorten decision time to 15 menit

### **For Virtual Training:**
- Use breakout rooms for different site cluster coordination
- Simulate video calls with site managers in emergency situations
- Use shared documents for real-time operational planning
- Include digital dashboard simulation with automated alerts and status updates
