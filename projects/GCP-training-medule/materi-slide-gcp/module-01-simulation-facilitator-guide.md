# 📚 **Facilitator Guide: Modul 1 Simulation**

## 🎯 **Learning Objectives**

Setelah menyelesaikan simulasi ini, peserta dapat:
1. Menerapkan prinsip CUKB dalam konteks modern
2. Mengidentifikasi ethical dilemmas dalam digital trials
3. Membuat keputusan berbasis risiko
4. Berkomunikasi efektif dalam krisis

---

## ⏰ **Recommended Timeline**

| **Phase** | **Duration** | **Activity** |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| **Setup** | 5 min | Briefing, role assignment, materials distribution |
| **Scenario Unfolding** | 15 min | Progressive revelation of challenges |
| **Decision Making** | 10 min | Group discussion and decision |
| **Debrief** | 15 min | Reflection and learning integration |

---

## 🎭 **Setup Instructions**

### **Room Configuration:**
- **U-shape seating** untuk diskusi optimal
- **Whiteboard/flipchart** untuk decision tree mapping
- **Projector** untuk menampilkan timeline updates
- **Breakout areas** (jika available) untuk role-specific discussions

### **Materials Preparation:**
1. **Printed handouts:**
   - Regulation quick reference (BPOM No. 8/2024 key points)
   - Stakeholder responsibility matrix
   - Decision tree template

2. **Technology needs:**
   - Timer for each phase
   - Optional: WhatsApp mockup on screen
   - Optional: Email template for participant responses

3. **Role cards (optional):**
   - PI card with specific challenges
   - CRC card with operational focus
   - Sub-I card with clinical perspective

---

## 🎬 **Step-by-Step Facilitation**

### **Phase 1: Introduction (5 menit)**

**Opening Script:**
> "Selamat pagi teman-teman. Hari ini kita akan melakukan simulasi yang berbeda. Anda akan berperan sebagai Principal Investigator untuk penelitian diabetes multi-situs di Indonesia. Tapi ini bukan penelitian biasa - ini adalah penelitian era digital dengan AI, telemedisin, dan teknologi canggih."
>
> "Tujuan kita bukan mencari jawaban 'benar' atau 'salah', tapi melatih cara berpikir kritis dalam menerapkan prinsip CUKB di situasi modern yang kompleks."

**Setup Activities:**
1. Bagikan role cards atau assign roles
2. Distribute handouts
3. Tekankan bahwa **keputusan harus dibuat dalam 15 menit** untuk simulasi real-time pressure

---

### **Phase 2: Scenario Unfolding (15 menit)**

**Progressive Revelation Strategy:**

#### **Minute 0-3: Initial Email**
- Tampilkan email sponsor di screen
- Berikan waktu untuk reading (1 menit)
- Ask: **"Apa initial thoughts Anda?"** (rapid responses)

#### **Minute 3-6: Cascade Problems**
- Introduce phone call from coordinator
- Add ET email
- Include IT report
- Show Manado WhatsApp

**Key Questions to Prompt:**
- "What's your immediate priority?"
- "Which regulation is most relevant now?"
- "Who needs to be contacted first?"

#### **Minute 6-10: Information Overload**
- Show WhatsApp screenshot
- Share investigation results
- Display BPOM alert email

**Facilitation Technique:**
- Increase time pressure announcements
- "Anda punya 5 menit sebelum pasien mulai complain"
- "Telepon dari BPOM bisa masuk kapan saja"

#### **Minute 10-15: Escalation**
- Media coverage update
- Legal threat notification
- Cyber security alert

**Build Emotional Pressure:**
- "Berita TV sudah tayang"
- "Pengacara pasien sudah siap-siap"
- "Data pasien sudah beredar"

---

### **Phase 3: Decision Point (10 menit)**

**Structured Decision Making:**

1. **Individual Reflection (2 menit):**
   - Write down personal recommendation
   - Identify key CUKP principles involved
   - Note primary risks

2. **Group Discussion (6 menit):**
   - Share individual perspectives
   - Attempt to reach consensus
   - Document decision rationale

3. **Final Decision (2 menit):**
   - Choose A, B, or C (or alternative)
   - Justify choice with CUKP principles
   - Plan immediate next steps

**Facilitation Prompts:**
- "Bagaimana keputusan Anda memenuhi Prinsip 1 (Etik)?"
- "Apa risiko dari keputusan ini untuk pasien?"
- "Bagaimana BPOM akan melihat keputusan Anda?"

---

### **Phase 4: Debrief (15 menit)**

**Structured Reflection:**

#### **Immediate Reactions (3 menit):**
- How did it feel making decisions under pressure?
- What was most challenging?
- Which stakeholder was hardest to satisfy?

#### **CUKB Principles Analysis (5 menit):**
- Map decisions to 11 principles
- Identify which principles conflicted
- Discuss how modern technology challenges traditional CUKB

#### **Lessons Learned (5 menit):**
- What would you do differently?
- What policies should be in place?
- How does this prepare you for real trials?

#### **Future Application (2 menit):**
- How will this change your practice?
- What questions remain?
- What additional training is needed?

---

## 🎯 **Key Teaching Points**

### **During Decision Phase:**
1. **Patient Safety First**: Always prioritize over timeline and budget
2. **Regulatory Compliance**: Not optional, even under pressure
3. **Documentation**: Everything must be documented in real-time
4. **Communication**: Critical for crisis management

### **During Debrief:**
1. **Complex Reality**: Modern trials have multiple competing priorities
2. **Critical Thinking**: Essential, not just knowledge recall
3. **Collaboration**: No single person has all answers
4. **Future Preparation**: This is reality, not simulation

---

## ⚠️ **Common Pitfalls & How to Address**

### **Pitfall 1: Analysis Paralysis**
**Symptom:** Groups can't make any decision
**Intervention:** "We need to make SOME decision in 2 minutes - what's the least harmful choice?"

### **Pitfall 2: Rushing Without Thinking**
**Symptom:** Immediate decision without considering alternatives
**Intervention:** "Stop. What CUKB principles are we applying here? What's the ethical foundation?"

### **Pitfall 3: Blaming Others**
**Symptom:** Focus on sponsor mistakes, not own responsibilities
**Intervention:** "Regardless of what the sponsor did wrong, what are YOUR responsibilities as PI?"

### **Pitfall 4: Forgetting Context**
**Symptom:** Solutions don't consider Indonesian context
**Intervention:** "How would this work in Manado? What about patients without smartphones?"

## 🔧 **Adaptation Options**

### **For Less Experienced Participants:**
- Reduce number of simultaneous challenges
- Provide more explicit guidance
- Extend decision time to 15 menit
- Use simpler scenario with fewer stakeholders

### **For Very Experienced Participants:**
- Add budget constraints to decision making
- Include media pressure with real-time social media simulation
- Add internal hospital politics complications
- Shorten decision time to 10 menit

### **For Virtual Delivery:**
- Use breakout rooms for role discussions
- Simulate phone calls through audio cues
- Use digital whiteboard for decision mapping
- Schedule debrief after short break

---

## 📊 **Assessment Guidance**

### **Participation Indicators:**
- Active engagement in decision discussions
- Reference to specific CUKP principles
- Consideration of multiple stakeholder perspectives
- Articulation of ethical reasoning

### **Critical Thinking Demonstrated:**
- Identifying competing priorities
- Evaluating options systematically
- Anticipating consequences
- Learning from experience

### **For Certification:**
- Minimum: Active participation
- Good: Ethical reasoning in discussions
- Excellent: Leadership in decision process + comprehensive debrief insights

## 🎁 **Bonus Activities (if time permits)**

### **Extended Scenario Extension:**
- Introduce a data breach 24 hours later
- Add a serious adverse event from improperly screened patient
- Include a surprise BPOM inspection

### **Role Reversal:**
- Have participants play different roles
- What if you were the sponsor? The regulator? The patient?

### **Real-World Connections:**
- Ask about actual similar experiences
- Discuss recent clinical trial controversies in Indonesia
- Connect to participants' actual work challenges

## 📝 **Post-Simulation Follow-up**

### **Immediate Actions:**
- Distribute summary of key learning points
- Share additional resources on digital trials
- Collect feedback for future improvements

### **Long-term Reinforcement:**
- Reference back to simulation in later modules
- Use similar decision-making frameworks throughout training
- Follow up on real-world application in 3-6 months

---

**Remember:** The goal isn't perfect decisions - it's developing critical thinking skills that will serve participants in real clinical trial challenges.
