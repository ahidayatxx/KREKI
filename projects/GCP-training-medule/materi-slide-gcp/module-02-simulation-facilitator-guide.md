# 📚 **Facilitator Guide: Modul 2 Simulation**

## 🎯 **Learning Objectives**

Setelah menyelesaikan simulasi ini, peserta dapat:
1. Menerapkan manajemen sumber daya dalam krisis multi-situs
2. Mengimplementasikan delegasi yang efektif di tim hybrid
3. Mengambil keputusan kepemimpinan di bawah tekanan
4. Menyeimbangkan inovasi teknologi dengan kepatuhan CUKB
5. Mengembangkan strategi komunikasi krisis

---

## ⏰ **Recommended Timeline**

| **Phase** | **Duration** | **Activity** |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| **Setup** | 5 min | Scenario briefing, role assignment |
| **Crisis Unfolding** | 20 min | Progressive revelation of escalating challenges |
| **Decision Making** | 15 min | Group discussions and prioritization |
| **Debrief** | 20 min | Leadership analysis and CUKB application |

---

## 🎭 **Setup Instructions**

### **Room Configuration:**
- **Multiple breakout areas** untuk "site teams"
- **Central crisis command table** untuk PI
- **Digital communication simulation** (WhatsApp/email displays)
- **Timeline board** untuk tracking events

### **Materials Preparation:**

#### **Printed Materials:**
1. **Role-specific packets:**
   - PI packet: Site maps, contact lists, regulatory timelines
   - CRC packet: Patient lists, protocol checklists
   - Site Manager packet: Local resources, staff schedules

2. **Visual aids:**
   - Map of 7 sites with connectivity status
   - Organizational chart of study team
   - Timeline of regulatory deadlines

3. **Reference materials:**
   - ICH E6(R3) investigator responsibilities summary
   - BPOM contact information
   - Emergency decision framework

### **Technology Setup:**
- **Projector:** Timeline updates and message feeds
- **Sound system:** For emergency "notifications"
- **Whiteboards:** Decision mapping and resource allocation
- **Timer:** Countdown to regulatory deadlines

## 🎬 **Step-by-Step Facilitation**

### **Phase 1: Introduction (5 menit)**

**Opening Script:**
> "Selamat pagi para investigator. Hari ini kita akan menghadapi skenario yang sangat realistis di era penelitian modern. Anda memimpin penelitian device kardiovaskular di 7 kota dengan teknologi remote monitoring yang canggih."
>
> "Tapi teknologi bukan berarti tanpa masalah. Anda akan menghadapi krisis bersamaan: kegagalan sistem, masalah staf, tekanan regulator, dan isu safety pasien."
>
> "Tujuan kita adalah menguji kemampuan leadership Anda dalam situasi kritis - bagaimana mengalokasikan sumber daya terbatas, mengambil keputusan sulit, dan mempertahankan integritas penelitian di bawah tekanan."

### **Setup Activities:**
1. Assign roles (1 PI, small groups for each site)
2. Distribute role-specific information packets
3. Explain the "real-time" nature of the simulation
4. Set expectations: decisions will have consequences

---

### **Phase 2: Crisis Unfolding (20 menit)**

**Progressive Escalation Strategy:**

#### **First 5 minutes - Initial Problems (08:00-08:30):**
- **Surabaya email:** Staffing and data issues
- **Remote site WhatsApps:** Technical and clinical problems
- **Data Manager crisis:** System-wide data integrity concerns

**Facilitation Prompts:**
- "What's your immediate reaction as PI?"
- "Which site needs your attention first?"
- "How do you verify the severity of each issue?"

#### **Next 5 minutes - Regulatory Pressure (08:30-09:00):**
- **Sponsor emergency call:** FDA safety report requirements
- **Technology assessment:** Multiple system failures confirmed
- **Competing priorities:** Patient safety vs regulatory deadlines

**Facilitation Questions:**
- "How do you prioritize patient safety vs. regulatory compliance?"
- "What information do you need from each site immediately?"
- "Who can you delegate to, and what can you delegate?"

#### **Next 5 minutes - Escalation (09:00-09:30):**
- **Hospital admin constraints:** Budget and staffing limitations
- **System-wide failures:** Ransomware attack confirmed
- **Media attention:** Patient complaints going viral

**Building Pressure Techniques:**
- Countdown timers for regulatory deadlines
- "You have 48 hours until FDA report is due"
- "Media reports are increasing exponentially"
- "Hospital IT says systems may be down for days"

#### **Final 5 minutes - Leadership Crisis (09:30-10:00):**
- **FDA ultimatum:** External audit requirement
- **Patient safety incident:** Life-threatening event at junior site
- **Team conflict:** Co-PI challenging leadership approach
- **Data security confirmation:** System compromise verified

**Crisis Peak:**
- "FDA clinical hold is imminent"
- "Patient's family threatening legal action"
- "Co-PI wants to abandon study"
- "All patient data may be compromised"

---

### **Phase 3: Decision Making (15 menit)**

**Structured Decision Process:**

#### **Minutes 1-3: Rapid Assessment**
- Quick prioritization of immediate threats
- Resource availability assessment
- Stakeholder communication planning

#### **Minutes 4-8: Strategy Development**
- Site-by-site response planning
- Delegation decisions with rationale
- Regulatory response preparation

#### **Minutes 9-12: Implementation Planning**
- Communication messages to key stakeholders
- Short-term operational adjustments
- Long-term corrective action planning

#### **Minutes 13-15: Final Decisions**
- Commit to specific action plan
- Justify decisions with CUKB principles
- Prepare for debrief reflection

**Facilitation Support:**
- Use decision tree framework on whiteboard
- Track time pressure with countdown displays
- Record key decisions for debrief discussion
- Note how participants handle conflicting priorities

---

### **Phase 4: Debrief (20 menit)**

#### **Part 1: Emotional Processing (3 menit)**
- "How did it feel making decisions under extreme pressure?"
- "Which challenge created the most anxiety?"
- "At what point did you feel most overwhelmed?"

#### **Part 2: Decision Analysis (7 menit)**
- Review the priority decisions made
- Discuss rationale behind resource allocation
- Evaluate delegation decisions
- Assess communication strategies

**Key Discussion Questions:**
- "How did you balance patient safety vs. regulatory compliance?"
- "When should you have escalated to sponsor/regulators?"
- "Which CUKB principles guided your decisions most?"

#### **Part 3: CUKB Application (7 menit)**
- Map decisions to ICH E6(R3) investigator responsibilities
- Discuss how traditional principles apply to digital trials
- Identify gaps in current CUKB guidance for digital scenarios

**Specific Focus Areas:**
- Section 4.1-4.12: Investigator responsibilities
- Section 5.1.3: Quality management systems
- Section 8.3.1: Financial disclosure and agreements

#### **Part 4: Learning Integration (3 menit)**
- "What would you do differently next time?"
- "What systems would you implement to prevent this crisis?"
- "How has this changed your view of modern trial management?"

## 🎯 **Key Teaching Points**

### **During Decision Phase:**
1. **Patient Safety Always First**: Even under regulatory pressure
2. **Clear Communication Essential**: Prevents panic and maintains trust
3. **Delegation Requires Competence Assessment**: Not just availability
4. **Digital Systems Need Redundancy**: Backup plans are critical

### **During Debrief:**
1. **Leadership vs. Management**: Leadership is about making tough decisions under uncertainty
2. **Crisis Builds Character**: Difficult situations reveal true leadership capabilities
3. **Modern Trials Need New Skills**: Traditional investigator training insufficient
4. **Collaboration Essential**: No single person can handle complex modern trials

---

## ⚠️ **Common Participant Responses & How to Guide**

### **Response 1: "I would hire more staff immediately"**
**Challenge:** Budget constraints in scenario
**Guiding Question:** "What if you cannot hire immediately? How do you work with existing resources?"

### **Response 2: "I would stop the study immediately"**
**Challenge:** Consider consequences for patients who rely on the device
**Guiding Question:** "What are the risks of stopping vs. continuing with safeguards?"

### **Response 3: "I would blame the sponsor/system vendors"**
**Challenge:** Focus on investigator responsibility
**Guiding Question:** "As PI, what is your responsibility regardless of who is at fault?"

### **Response 4: "I would handle everything myself"**
**Challenge:** Impracticality of single-person response
**Guiding Question:** "What can you realistically accomplish alone? What must you delegate?"

### **Response 5: "This would never happen at my site"**
**Challenge:** Overconfidence leads to lack of preparation
**Guiding Question:** "What systems do you have in place to prevent similar crises?"

## 🔧 **Adaptation Options**

### **For Less Experienced Investigators:**
- Reduce number of simultaneous crises
- Provide more structured decision framework
- Extend decision time to 20 menit
- Focus on single-site scenario first

### **For Very Experienced PIs:**
- Add sponsor financial pressure
- Include patient advocacy group involvement
- Add competitor trial interference
- Shorten decision time to 10 menit
- Include media interview scenario

### **For Virtual Training:**
- Use breakout rooms for site teams
- Simulate video calls with regulator
- Use shared documents for real-time collaboration
- Include email simulation with timed responses

## 📊 **Assessment Criteria**

### **Leadership Competencies:**
1. **Decision Quality**: Evidence-based, prioritized decisions
2. **Communication**: Clear, timely, appropriate messaging
3. **Delegation**: Appropriate task assignment with oversight
4. **Crisis Management**: Calm, effective emergency response

### **CUKB Compliance:**
1. **Resource Management**: Adequate provision and supervision
2. **Protocol Adherence**: Maintaining compliance during crisis
3. **Documentation**: Proper recording of deviations and actions
4. **Safety Reporting**: Appropriate and timely adverse event reporting

### **Digital Era Adaptation:**
1. **Technology Literacy**: Understanding digital system limitations
2. **Remote Team Management**: Effective virtual leadership
3. **Contingency Planning**: Backup systems and procedures
4. **Data Integrity**: Protection during system failures

## 🔍 **Facilitator Notes**

### **Key Moments to Watch For:**
1. **Initial overwhelm assessment:** How participants process multiple crises
2. **Prioritization decisions:** Evidence of systematic thinking vs. panic
3. **Delegation quality:** Appropriateness of task assignment
4. **Communication strategies:** How messages are crafted and delivered
5. **Ethical reasoning:** How patient safety is prioritized

### **Common Learning Gaps:**
1. **Underestimating time pressure:** Real crisis moves faster than simulation
2. **Over-reliance on technology:** Assuming digital systems always work
3. **Insufficient contingency planning:** Not having backup systems
4. **Poor stakeholder communication:** Not considering all audiences

### **Success Indicators:**
- Participants make decisions based on CUKB principles
- Effective delegation with appropriate oversight
- Clear, professional communication strategies
- Recognition of personal limitations and need for help

---

**Remember:** This simulation pushes participants beyond comfort zones to reveal true leadership capabilities. The goal isn't perfect crisis management but developing reflective practitioners who learn from experience.
