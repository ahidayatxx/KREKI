# 📚 **Facilitator Guide: Modul 3 Simulation**

## 🎯 **Learning Objectives**

Setelah menyelesaikan simulasi ini, peserta dapat:
1. Mengelola multi-jurisdictional regulatory requirements yang kompleks
2. Menerapkan protocol amendments dengan teknologi AI
3. Menavigasi digital regulatory processes dan virtual inspections
4. Membuat keputusan kepatuhan dalam situasi ambigu
5. Mengkoordinasikan stakeholder responses dalam krisis regulasi

---

## ⏰ **Recommended Timeline**

| **Phase** | **Duration** | **Activity** |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| **Setup** | 10 min | Digital regulatory context briefing, role assignment |
| **Crisis Unfolding** | 25 min | Multi-jurisdictional regulatory escalation |
| **Decision Making** | 20 menit | Strategy development and stakeholder coordination |
| **Debrief** | 20 menit | Regulatory innovation analysis and future preparedness |

## 🎭 **Setup Instructions**

### **Room Configuration:**
- **Global Command Center** layout untuk regulatory coordination
- **Multiple regional stations** (US FDA, EMA, Japan PMDA, Indonesia BPOM)
- **Digital dashboard** untuk real-time deadline tracking
- **Virtual meeting simulation** area for remote ET interactions

### **Materials Preparation:**

#### **Printed Materials:**
1. **Regulatory Framework Packets:**
   - FDA traditional submission requirements
   - EMA digital regulations
   - Japan PMDA conservative approach guidelines
   - Indonesia BPOM digital innovation framework

2. **Timeline Management Tools:**
   - Global deadline calendar with different time zones
   - Regulatory decision flowcharts
   - Amendment submission checklists per jurisdiction

3. **Communication Templates:**
   - Stakeholder response templates
   - Media question preparation guides
   - Site implementation instructions

### **Technology Simulation:**
- **Multiple "email feeds"** showing regulator communications
- **Digital dashboard countdown** clocks for each jurisdiction
- **Social media monitoring** simulation (hashtags trending)
- **Virtual meeting platform** for ET reviews

## 🎬 **Step-by-Step Facilitation**

### **Phase 1: Introduction (10 menit)**

**Opening Script:**
> "Selamat pagi para regulatory professionals. Hari ini kita akan menghadapi skenario yang belum pernah terjadi sebelumnya dalam sejarah penelitian klinis: krisis regulasi global di era digital dan AI."
>
> "Anda memimpin adaptive platform trial pertama di Indonesia dengan AI-driven protocol modifications. Tiba-tiba, sistem AI mendeteksi sinyal keamanan dan efikasi yang memerlukan amendemen protokol global dalam 14 hari."
>
> "Tantangannya: setiap regulator memiliki persyaratan berbeda untuk validasi AI. FDA wants traditional validation, EMA wants risk assessment, Japan wants traditional methods, while BPOM is pioneering digital review."
>
> "Tujuan kita adalah menguji kemampuan navigasi regulasi global yang kompleks sesekaligus mempertahankan akses pasien terhadap treatment yang efektif. Ini bukan hanya tentang compliance - ini tentang masa depan penelitian klinis."

### **Setup Activities:**
1. Assign roles (Global Regulatory Manager, Local Regulatory Specialists, Site PIs)
2. Distribute jurisdiction-specific regulatory requirement packets
3. Explain the "global coordination" nature of the simulation
4. Set up the multi-timezone deadline dashboard

---

### **Phase 2: Crisis Unfolding (25 menit)**

**Progressive Global Escalation Strategy:**

#### **First 7 minutes - Initial Regulatory Pressure (09:00-09:30):**
- **Global head email:** Multiple regulator requests simultaneously
- **BPOM digital system alert:** AI submission complexity flags
- **ET virtual meeting request:** Immediate review requirement

**Facilitation Prompts:**
- "How do you prioritize conflicting regulatory requirements?"
- "Which jurisdiction's requirements should you address first?"
- "How do you coordinate global response from Indonesian headquarters?"

#### **Next 8 minutes - Technology System Challenges (09:30-10:30):**
- **Digital platform failures:** AI submission system instability
- **Site crisis reports:** Conflicting data from AI vs. traditional methods
- **Social media pressure:** Patient stories going viral

**Building Pressure Techniques:**
- Countdown timers for different regulator deadlines
- "FDA deadline: 7 days remaining"
- "EMA requesting additional data within 48 hours"
- "Patient families staging protests at Jakarta site"

#### **Final 10 minutes - Escalation Peak (10:30-11:30):**
- **Internal team conflicts:** Global disagreements on strategy
- **Site implementation chaos:** Multiple protocol versions confusion
- **Media attention:** International news coverage developing
- **Technology failures:** Backup systems not functioning

**Crisis Peak Elements:**
- "FDA threatening clinical hold if traditional validation not provided"
- "Patient advocates demanding continued access to effective treatment"
- "Journalists questioning AI methodology in clinical trials"
- "Technical team cannot access backup regulatory submission systems"

---

### **Phase 3: Decision Making (20 menit)**

**Strategic Decision Framework:**

#### **Minutes 1-5: Regulatory Strategy Development**
- Assess regulatory risk across all jurisdictions
- Evaluate AI data validity requirements
- Determine amendment approach (traditional, hybrid, or digital-first)
- Plan timeline coordination strategy

#### **Minutes 6-12: Stakeholder Coordination**
- Internal team consensus building
- Site implementation planning
- Patient communication strategy
- Media response preparation

#### **Minutes 13-17: Implementation Planning**
- Document submission preparation per jurisdiction
- Site training for protocol changes
- Ethical review coordination
- Backup system activation

#### **Minutes 18-20: Final Decision Commitment**
- Choose regulatory strategy
- Prepare implementation timeline
- Assign responsibility areas
- Prepare for debrief

**Facilitation Support:**
- Use decision matrix on whiteboard comparing approach options
- Track stakeholder positions and conflicts
- Record strategy rationale for debrief discussion
- Note how participants handle uncertainty

---

### **Phase 4: Debrief (20 menit)**

#### **Part 1: Decision Analysis (5 menit)**
- "Which regulatory strategy provided the best balance?"
- "How did you handle conflicting jurisdictional requirements?"
- "When did you trust AI data vs. traditional validation?"

#### **Part 2: Global Coordination Challenges (5 menit)**
- "How did you manage different regulatory deadlines?"
- "What worked in coordinating with multiple jurisdictions?"
- "How did you prevent site confusion during protocol changes?"

#### **Part 3: Innovation vs. Tradition Balance (5 menit)**
- "When should new technologies override traditional regulatory processes?"
- "What evidence was sufficient for regulatory decisions?"
- "How should regulators adapt to AI and digital innovations?"

#### **Part 4: Future Regulatory Evolution (5 menit)**
- "What regulatory changes does this scenario suggest are needed?"
- "How should clinical trials be designed for future regulatory environments?"
- "What skills are essential for regulatory professionals in the digital era?"

---

## 🎯 **Key Teaching Points**

### **During Decision Phase:**
1. **Regulatory agility is essential** - Traditional processes may be too slow for modern trials
2. **Multi-jurisdictional coordination requires systematic approach** - Cannot rely on ad-hoc responses
3. **Patient safety must drive regulatory decisions** - Even when facing innovative technologies
4. **Technology validation frameworks are evolving** - Need both traditional and modern validation

### **During Debrief:**
1. **Regulatory innovation must be balanced with patient protection** - New technologies need new safeguards
2. **Global harmonization requires technology enablement** - Traditional processes insufficient for global trials
3. **Stakeholder communication becomes more critical in complex regulatory environments** - Multiple audiences require tailored messages
4. **Future regulatory professionals need new skill sets** - Digital literacy, AI assessment, global coordination

---

## ⚠️ **Common Participant Responses & Guidance**

### **Response 1: "Follow traditional requirements and ignore AI"**
**Challenge:** Patients lose access to potentially life-saving treatment
**Guiding Question:** "What if the AI data shows the treatment is saving lives? How do you balance innovation with traditional validation?"

### **Response 2: "Trust AI completely and proceed immediately"**
**Challenge:** Regulatory non-compliance risks and patient safety concerns
**Guiding Question:** "What validation steps are needed before trusting AI-driven protocol changes? How do you ensure patient safety?"

### **Response 3: "Handle each jurisdiction separately"**
**Challenge:** Protocol fragmentation and global coordination failure
**Guiding Question:** "How do you maintain study consistency when following different protocols? What does this mean for global drug development?"

### **Response 4: "Focus only on Indonesian requirements"**
**Challenge:** Global trial coordination and regulatory alignment
**Guiding Question:** "How does Indonesia-specific approach impact global trial validity? What about other countries' patients?"

### **Response 5: "Wait for regulators to provide clear guidance"**
**Challenge:** Missed opportunity for innovation and patient benefit
**Guiding Question:** "How can industry drive regulatory innovation responsibly? What role should sponsors play in shaping modern regulations?"

## 🔧 **Adaptation Options**

### **For Less Experienced Participants:**
- Reduce number of jurisdictions (focus on Indonesia and one other)
- Provide more structured decision frameworks
- Extend decision time to 30 menit
- Focus on single regulatory challenge rather than multiple simultaneous

### **For Very Experienced Regulatory Professionals:**
- Add more complex AI validation challenges
- Include investor and board member pressure scenarios
- Add political and public health ministry involvement
- Include competitive pharmaceutical company interference
- Shorten decision time to 15 menit

### **For Virtual Training:**
- Use breakout rooms for different jurisdiction teams
- Simulate virtual regulator meetings with role-play
- Use shared documents for real-time amendment drafting
- Include email simulation with automated regulator responses
