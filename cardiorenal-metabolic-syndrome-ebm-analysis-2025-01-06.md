- Treats T2DM, HFrEF, and CKD simultaneously

## CONTRAINDICATIONS & PRECAUTIONS

### SGLT2 Inhibitors:
⚠️ **Avoid if:**
- Type 1 diabetes
- eGFR <20 mL/min/1.73m²
- Active DKA
- Pregnancy/breastfeeding

⚠️ **Use with caution:**
- History of DKA (this patient) - monitor carefully
- Volume depletion - adjust diuretics
- recurrent genital infections

### ARNI:
⚠️ **Avoid if:**
- History of angioedema with ACEi/ARB
- Concomitant ACEi/ARB (washout 36 hours)
- Severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh C)
- Pregnancy

⚠️ **Use with caution:**
- K+ >5 mEq/L
- Symptomatic hypotension - adjust diuretics

### Beta-Blockers:
⚠️ **Avoid if:**
- Severe bradycardia (<50 bpm)
- Second/third degree AV block without pacemaker
- Active bronchospasm (use cardioselective or carvedilol)

### DOACs:
⚠️ **Avoid if:**
- Mechanical heart valves
- Moderate-to-severe mitral stenosis
- Active severe bleeding

⚠️ **Use with caution:**
- CrCl <15 mL/min (apixaban still preferred over warfarin)
- Concomitant strong P-gp inhibitors or inducers

### Finerenone:
⚠️ **Avoid if:**
- Concomitant ACEi/ARB AND strong CYP3A4 inhibitors
- K+ >5.0 mEq/L
- Pregnancy

### Statins:
⚠️ **Avoid if:**
- Active liver disease
- Pregnancy

⚠️ **Use with caution:**
- History of myopathy
- Concomitant strong CYP3A4 inhibitors (adjust dose)

### Critical Interactions:
1. **ARNI + Loop Diuretics:** Enhanced hypotension, renal dysfunction - monitor closely
2. **ARNI + MRA (Eplerenone/Finerenone):** Hyperkalemia risk - monitor K+ weekly initially
3. **SGLT2i + Diuretics:** Volume depletion - may need diuretic dose reduction
4. **DOACs + Antiplatelets:** Increased bleeding - use only if clear indication (this patient has ASCVD)
5. **Statins + Finerenone:** No significant interaction

### Moderate Interactions:
1. **Beta-blockers + SGLT2i:** Potential for hypoglycemia awareness blunting
2. **GLP-1 RA + Delayed gastric emptying:** May affect oral medications - space administration
3. **Thiazides + Finerenone:** Enhanced hyperkalemia risk

## POTENTIAL ADVERSE EFFECTS & MANAGEMENT

### Hyperkalemia (K+ >5.5 mEq/L):
**Caused by:** ARNI, MRA, Finerenone, SGLT2i (may lower risk)

**Management:**
- K+ 5.0-5.5: Monitor, consider dietary K+ restriction
- K+ 5.5-6.0: Hold MRA/finerenone, recheck in 1 week
- K+ >6.0: Hold all RAAS/MRA agents, consider potassium binder
- Restart when K+ <5.0

**Prevention:**
- Potassium binders (patiromer, SZC) enable continuation of RAAS/MRA
- Low-potassium diet
- Avoid NSAIDs

### Worsening Renal Function:
**Definition:** >30% eGFR decline or <50% of baseline

**Management:**
- <30% decline: Continue medications, monitor closely
- 30-50% decline: Hold SGLT2i temporarily if acute illness, otherwise continue
- >50% decline: Hold ACEi/ARB/ARNI/MRA, evaluate for volume depletion
- Don't discontinue SGLT2i for expected eGFR dip (<10% in first month)

### Volume Depletion:
**Caused by:** SGLT2i + Diuretics

**Management:**
- Reduce diuretic dose temporarily
- Ensure adequate hydration
- Monitor orthostatic vitals

### DKA with SGLT2i:
**Risk Factors:** Poor glycemic control (HbA1c 11.2%), acute illness, alcohol, reduced caloric intake

**Management:**
- Educate patient on symptoms (nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, Kussmaul breathing)
- Check ketones if symptomatic
- Temporarily hold SGLT2i during acute illness
- May need to continue with caution if DKA was precipitated by acute illness

### GI Effects with GLP-1 RA:
**Common:** Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation

**Management:**
- Start at lowest dose
- Titrate slowly every 4 weeks
- Take with food
- Consider antiemetic temporarily if needed
- Usually improves over time

## KEY TAKEAWAYS FOR CLINICIANS

### Top 10 Evidence-Based Recommendations:

1. **SGLT2 Inhibitors are foundational** - Benefits for HFrEF, CKD, and T2DM are independent and additive
2. **ARNI over ACEi/ARB** - 20% mortality benefit, safe in CKD 3b
3. **DOACs preferred over warfarin** - Superior safety and efficacy in CKD
4. **GLP-1 RAs for additional cardiorenal protection** - FLOW trial practice-changing
5. **Finerenone in T2DM + CKD** - Additive to SGLT2i, lower hyperkalemia than steroidal MRAs
6. **High-intensity statins for ASCVD** - Safe and effective in CKD 3b
7. **Complete GDMT for HFrEF** - ARNI + Beta-blocker + MRA + SGLT2i (Class I, Level A)
8. **Aggressive BP control** - Target <130/80 mmHg for CKD + proteinuria
9. **Close monitoring in first 3 months** - K+, renal function, volume status
10. **Multidisciplinary care** - Cardiology, nephrology, endocrinology collaboration

### Red Flags Requiring Immediate Action:
- 🚨 K+ >6.0 mEq/L → Hold all RAAS/MRA, evaluate
- 🚨 eGFR decline >50% → Hold ACEi/ARB/ARNI, evaluate volume status
- 🚨 Symptomatic hypotension → Reduce diuretics, hold or reduce ARNI
- 🚨 Signs of DKA → Check ketones, hold SGLT2i if positive
- 🚨 Acute decompensation → Urgent evaluation, may need hospitalization

### When to Refer:
- Worsening renal function despite optimization
- Recurrent hyperkalemia preventing GDMT
- Need for advanced HF therapies (transplant, LVAD, palliative)
- Complex medication management issues
