## Practical Considerations

### Dosing & Administration

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Semaglutide** | Start 0.25 mg weekly SC → titrate to 2.4 mg over 16-20 weeks |
| **Tirzepatide** | Start 2.5 mg weekly SC → titrate to 10-15 mg max over 4-12 weeks |
| **Frequency** | Once weekly subcutaneous injection (any time of day, with/without food) |
| **Administration** | Abdomen, thigh, or upper arm; rotate injection sites |
| **Monitoring** | Weight, waist circumference, fasting lipids, HbA1c, renal function at baseline and every 3 months |

### Contraindications

**Absolute:**
- Personal/family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC)
- Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2)
- Pregnancy (discontinue at least 2 months before planned pregnancy)
- Hypersensitivity to semaglutide or any excipients

**Relative (caution):**
- History of pancreatitis
- Diabetic retinopathy (monitor for worsening)
- Gallbladder disease (increased risk of cholelithiasis)
- Concurrent medications with narrow therapeutic index

### Special Populations

| Population | Considerations |
|------------|----------------|
| **Elderly (≥65 years)** | No dose adjustment; monitor renal function more frequently |
| **Renal impairment** | No dose adjustment for mild-moderate; not recommended for severe (eGFR <30) |
| **Hepatic impairment** | No dose adjustment for mild-moderate; limited data for severe |
| **Pregnancy** | Discontinue before conception; insulin preferred during pregnancy |
| **Breastfeeding** | Limited data; consider benefit-risk ratio |

## Key Takeaways

### For Clinicians

1. **Primary Recommendation:** For a 58-year-old male with BMI 32, dyslipidemia, and insulin resistance, GLP-1 receptor agonists (semaglutide 2.4 mg) or dual GIP/GLP-1 agonists (tirzepatide) are **strongly recommended** as first-line pharmacotherapy in combination with lifestyle modification. Evidence from STEP and SURMOUNT trials demonstrates 14.9-18.5% weight loss with significant metabolic benefits.

2. **Evidence Quality:** This recommendation is based on **8 high-quality studies** (1 meta-analysis, 7 phase 3 RCTs) published 2023-2025, including large sample sizes (n=645 to n=1,311) from the STEP and SURMOUNT trial programs. All studies were published in peer-reviewed journals with high impact factors.

3. **Patient Selection:** Ideal candidates have BMI ≥30 (or ≥27 with weight-related comorbidities), metabolic syndrome features (insulin resistance, dyslipidemia), and have failed adequate trials of lifestyle modification alone. This patient profile matches the inclusion criteria from the STEP trials perfectly.

4. **Monitoring Requirements:**
   - **Baseline:** Weight, waist circumference, fasting lipids, HbA1c, renal function, TSH, gallbladder ultrasound if symptomatic
   - **Every 3 months:** Weight, waist circumference, fasting lipids, HbA1c, renal function
   - **Medication side effects:** GI tolerance assessment at each visit
   - **Cardiovascular risk:** Monitor for improvements in blood pressure, lipid profile, inflammatory markers

5. **Alternative Options:** If GLP-1/GIP agonists are contraindicated or not tolerated, consider:
   - Orlistat (lower efficacy, OTC availability)
   - Phentermine/topiramate ER (contraindicated with uncontrolled hypertension)
   - Bariatric surgery (BMI ≥35 with comorbidities or ≥40)
   - Intensive lifestyle intervention alone (expected 5-10% weight loss)

### Areas of Uncertainty

- **Long-term cardiovascular outcomes:** SELECT trial (semaglutide for primary prevention of MACE) ongoing; results expected 2025-2026
- **Comparative effectiveness:** Head-to-head trials of semaglutide vs tirzepatide for weight loss and metabolic outcomes ongoing
- **Optimal treatment duration:** Unclear whether indefinite therapy is required or if tapering after target weight is feasible
- **Real-world adherence:** Trial settings show higher efficacy than real-world practice (9.22% vs 14.9% weight loss per Krishnan 2025)
- **Cost-effectiveness:** High medication costs may limit access; health economic analyses needed for metabolic syndrome populations
