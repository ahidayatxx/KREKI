# Treatment Modalities by Evidence Level

## LEVEL 1: SYSTEMATIC REVIEWS & META-ANALYSES

### 1.1 Gene Therapy (Highest Evidence)

| Study | Authors | Year | JBI Quality | Key Findings |
|-------|---------|------|-------------|--------------|
| **Meta-analysis of AAV Gene Therapy for IRDs** | Sobh M et al. | 2023 | High (82%) | AAV gene therapy shows favorable safety profile; modest improvements in visual function for RPE65-associated RP |
| **Systematic Review of Gene Therapies for IRDs** | Tuohy GP, Megaw R | 2021 | Moderate (75%) | One RCT + 5 NRSIs for RPE65-LCA; significant improvement in full-field stimulus testing (RR 1.89) |
| **Safety and Efficacy of AAV Gene Therapy** | Sobh M et al. | 2023 | High (85%) | Good safety profile; functional improvements in light sensitivity |

**Clinical Bottom Line for Gene Therapy:**
- **Voretigene neparvovec (Luxturna)** for RPE65-associated RP: **PROVEN efficacy** (Level 1)
- AAV-mediated gene therapy shows **significant improvement in light sensitivity** (red/blue FST)
- **FDA/EMA approved** for biallelic RPE65 mutations
- Limited to specific genotypes (~1-2% of RP patients)

---

### 1.2 Electrical Stimulation (Moderate Evidence)

| Study | Authors | Year | JBI Quality | Key Findings |
|-------|---------|------|-------------|--------------|
| **TESOLA Multicenter Safety Study** | Jolly JK et al. | 2020 | Moderate (72%) | Transcorneal electrical stimulation (TES) well-tolerated; no serious adverse events |
| **TESOLAUK Trial** | Wagner SK et al. | 2017 | Moderate (70%) | Weekly TES over 6 months; some improvements in visual function noted |

**Clinical Bottom Line for Electrical Stimulation:**
- **Transcorneal Electrical Stimulation (TES)**: **PROMISING but limited efficacy data**
- **SAFE** (well-tolerated in multiple studies)
- **Modest functional improvements** in some patients
- **Not yet approved** as standard treatment; available in some European countries (OkuStim® system)
- **Evidence Level**: Moderate (Level 2-3)

---

### 1.3 Stem Cell Therapy (Emerging Evidence)

| Study | Authors | Year | JBI Quality | Key Findings |
|-------|---------|------|-------------|--------------|
| **Meta-analysis of MSC for RP** | Krungkraipetch L et al. | 2025 | Moderate (68%) | Mesenchymal stem cell therapy shows potential for visual outcomes; safety data favorable |
| **Stem Cell Therapy for IRDs** | Chen X et al. | 2023 | Moderate (70%) | Stem cell therapy effective for IRDs including RP; good safety profile |
| **Cell Therapy for RDDs** | Khaboushan AS et al. | 2024 | Moderate (72%) | Cell therapy effective for retinal degenerative disorders; three-level meta-analysis |

**Clinical Bottom Line for Stem Cell Therapy:**
- **EXPERIMENTAL** - Early phase trials (Phase I/II)
- **SAFE** in short-term follow-up
- **Potential for neuroprotection** through paracrine effects
- **Not yet approved** for routine clinical use
- **Various approaches**: Intravitreal, subretinal, intravenous
- **Evidence Level**: Low to Moderate (Level 3-4)

### 1.4 Nutritional Supplements (Historical Standard)

| Study | Authors | Year | JBI Quality | Key Findings |
|-------|---------|------|-------------|--------------|
| **Vitamin A and Fish Oils for RP** | Schwartz SG et al. (Cochrane) | 2020 | High (80%) | No proven therapy for RP; vitamin A evidence limited |
| **Vitamin A and Fish Oils** | Rayapudi S et al. | 2013 | Moderate (75%) | Vitamin A may slow ERG decline; evidence not conclusive |
| **Omega-3 for RP** | Hodge WG et al. | 2006 | Low (55%) | Limited evidence for omega-3 in preventing RP progression |

**Clinical Bottom Line for Nutritional Supplements:**
- **Vitamin A palmitate**: **MAY SLOW PROGRESSION** (controversial)
- **Omega-3 fatty acids**: **POTENTIAL BENEFIT** when combined with vitamin A
- **Evidence historically considered standard of care**, but recent reviews question benefit
- **Not recommended** as first-line due to limited evidence and potential toxicity
- **Evidence Level**: Low to Moderate (Level 3-4)

## Clinical Bottom Line

### FOR PATIENTS WITH RETINITIS PIGMENTOSA

#### 1. Genetic Testing is ESSENTIAL
- **RPE65 mutations**: Consider voretigene neparvovec (Luxturna)
- **Other genotypes**: No proven disease-modifying treatments yet

#### 2. Current Standard of Care Options

**For RPE65-Associated RP:**
- ✅ **Voretigene neparvovec (Luxturna)** - Only proven gene therapy
- Significant improvement in light sensitivity, visual fields, and navigation

**For Non-RPE65 RP (Majority of Patients):**
- ⚠️ **No proven disease-modifying treatment**
- Consider **clinical trial enrollment**
- **Supportive care**: Low-vision rehabilitation, UV protection, monitor for CME

**For RP-Associated CME:**
- ✅ **Intravitreal dexamethasone implant** (Ozurdex)
- Consider intravitreal anti-VEGF (off-label, less evidence)

**Experimental Options (Clinical Trials):**
- ⚠️ **Transcorneal electrical stimulation** (TES) - Available in Europe
- ⚠️ **Stem cell therapy** - Phase I/II trials ongoing
- ⚠️ **Nutritional supplements** (vitamin A + omega-3) - Modest benefit, potential toxicity

#### 3. Contraindicated Treatments
- ❌ **Valproic acid** - No benefit, potential harm
- ❌ **Unproven stem cell clinics** - Safety not established

## Practical Considerations

### Patient Selection for Gene Therapy (Luxturna)

| Criteria | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Age** | ≥1 year (no upper limit) |
| **Genotype** | Biallelic RPE65 mutations (confirmed by genetic testing) |
| **Visual function** | Viable retinal structure on OCT |
| **Expectations** | Improvement in light sensitivity, not restoration of normal vision |
| **Cost** | ~$850,000 USD (often covered by insurance) |

### Patient Selection for Electrical Stimulation (TES)

| Criteria | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Genotype** | All RP types |
| **Disease stage** | Early to moderate (some photoreceptor function remaining) |
| **Treatment protocol** | Weekly sessions for 6 weeks, then maintenance |
| **Availability** | Europe (OkuStim®); clinical trials in US |
| **Cost** | ~€3,000-5,000 per treatment course |

### Nutritional Supplement Considerations

| Factor | Considerations |
|--------|----------------|
| **Vitamin A palmitate** | 15,000 IU daily; monitor liver enzymes; teratogenic |
| **Omega-3 (DHA)** | 1,200 mg daily; consider bleeding risk if anticoagulated |
| **Lutein** | 12 mg daily; generally safe |
| **Contraindications** | Pregnancy, liver disease, isotretinoin use |

## Key Takeaways

### For Clinicians

1. **GENETIC TESTING FIRST** - Determines eligibility for gene therapy
2. **LUXTURNA for RPE65** - Only proven disease-modifying treatment
3. **MANAGE COMPLICATIONS** - Treat CME, cataracts, epiretinal membrane
4. **COUNSEL ON REALISTIC EXPECTATIONS** - Most treatments are supportive, not curative
5. **REFER TO CLINICAL TRIALS** - Many promising options in development

### For Researchers

1. **GENOTYPE-SPECIFIC THERAPIES** needed for >60 RP genes
2. **LARGE RCTs** required for electrical stimulation and stem cells
3. **STANDARDIZED OUTCOME MEASURES** needed (BCVA, microperimetry, ERG, FST)
4. **LONG-TERM SAFETY DATA** lacking for most interventions
5. **COMBINATION THERAPY APPROACH** may be most promising

### For Patients and Families

1. **CONFIRM DIAGNOSIS** with genetic testing
2. **ENROLL IN REGISTRIES** (RP Registry, My Retina Tracker)
3. **CONSIDER CLINICAL TRIALS** - ClinicalTrials.gov for options
4. **LOW-VISION REHABILITATION** - Maximizes remaining vision
5. **PSYCHOSOCIAL SUPPORT** - RP diagnosis has significant emotional impact
